Concept note-2: -A plutonic rock is generally coarsely crystalline (phaneritic) and is formed during a massive geologic intrusion of magma. In fact, outcropping (or daylighting) plutonic rocks are geologic features that have been uplifted to the ground surface, long after solidifying underground.

Concept note-3: -Plutonic Rocks Examples The most common rock types in plutons include monzonite, granite, granodiorite, tonalite, and quartz diorite.
