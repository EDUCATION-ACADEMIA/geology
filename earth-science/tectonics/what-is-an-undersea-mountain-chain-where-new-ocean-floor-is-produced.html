What is an undersea mountain chain where new ocean floor is produced?

(A) Rift valley

(B) Deep-ocean trench

(C) ** Mid-ocean ridge

(D) Transform bounda

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Mid-ocean ridges occur along divergent plate boundaries, where new ocean floor is created as the Earth’s tectonic plates spread apart. As the plates separate, molten rock rises to the seafloor, producing enormous volcanic eruptions of basalt.

Concept note-2: -mid-ocean ridge-the undersea mountain chain where new ocean floor is produced; a divergent plate boundary.

Concept note-3: -Undersea mountain ranges are mountain ranges that are mostly or entirely underwater, and specifically under the surface of an ocean. If originated from current tectonic forces, they are often referred to as a mid-ocean ridge. In contrast, if formed by past above-water volcanism, they are known as a seamount chain.

Concept note-4: -The Mid-Atlantic Ridge (MAR) is a mostly underwater mountain range in the Atlantic Ocean that runs from 87°N-about 333km south of the North Pole-to subantarctic Bourvet island at 54°S.
