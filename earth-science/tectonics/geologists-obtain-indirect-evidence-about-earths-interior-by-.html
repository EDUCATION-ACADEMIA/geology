Geologists obtain indirect evidence about Earth’s interior by ____

(A) measuring pressure at the surface

(B) ** studying seismic waves

(C) drilling through each layer

(D) estimation of temperature in the layers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -To study Earth’s interior, geologists also use an indirect method. But instead of knocking on walls, they use seismic waves. When earthquakes occur they produce seismic waves. Geologists record the seismic waves and study how they travel through Earth.

Concept note-2: -These recordings made by a seismograph are called seismograms. Seismic waves travel at different speeds when they pass through different types of material, so by studying seismograms, scientists can learn a lot about Earth’s internal structure.

Concept note-3: -The geologists use rock samples as direct evidence and seismic waves as indirect evidence to study the Earth’s structure.
