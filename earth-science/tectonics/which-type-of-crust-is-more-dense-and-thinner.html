Which type of crust is more dense and thinner?

(A) Continental

(B) Asthenosphere

(C) ** Oceanic

(D) Lithosphere

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Oceanic crust is the part of the Earth’s crust that makes up the seafloor. It’s thinner, denser, and simpler in structure than the continental crust.

Concept note-2: -Oceanic crust is generally composed of dark-colored rocks called basalt and gabbro. It is thinner and denser than continental crust, which is made of light-colored rocks called andesite and granite.

Concept note-3: -There are two kinds of crust. Oceanic crust is thinner and denser than continental crust. Continental crust is much thicker than oceanic crust. It is 35 kilometers (22 miles) thick on average.

Concept note-4: -Continental crust is broadly granitic in composition and, with a density of about 2.7 grams per cubic cm, is somewhat lighter than oceanic crust, which is basaltic (i.e., richer in iron and magnesium than granite) in composition and has a density of about 2.9 to 3 grams per cubic cm.

Concept note-5: -It is thinner than continental crust, or sial, generally less than 10 kilometers thick; however, it is denser, having a mean density of about 3.0 grams per cubic centimeter as opposed to continental crust which has a density of about 2.7 grams per cubic centimeter.
