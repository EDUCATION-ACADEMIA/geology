This scientist built most of the evidence for continental drift.

(A) ** Alfred Wegener

(B) Fred Calfred

(C) Sean Carroll

(D) John Williams

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It wasn’t until the early 20th century that German scientist Alfred Wegener suggested that the Earth’s continents were drifting. He called this movement Continental Drift.

Concept note-2: -The theory of continental drift is most associated with the scientist Alfred Wegener. In the early 20th century, Wegener published a paper explaining his theory that the continental landmasses were “drifting” across the Earth, sometimes plowing through oceans and into each other.

Concept note-3: -Wegener used fossil evidence to support his continental drift hypothesis. The fossils of these organisms are found on lands that are now far apart. Grooves and rock deposits left by ancient glaciers are found today on different continents very close to the equator.

Concept note-4: -Alfred Wegener in Greenland. Plate tectonics is the theory that Earth’s land masses are in constant motion. The realization that Earth’s land masses move was first proposed by Alfred Wegener, which he called continental drift.

Concept note-5: -Wegener was a German meteorologist, geophysicist and polar researcher. In 1915 he published ‘The Origin of Continents and Oceans’, which outlined his theory of Continental Drift. Wegener was a member of four expeditions to Greenland.
