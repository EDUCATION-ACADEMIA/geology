The thickest layer

(A) crust

(B) inner core

(C) ** mantle

(D) outer core

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The mantle is the mostly solid bulk of Earth’s interior. The mantle lies between Earth’s dense, super-heated core and its thin outer layer, the crust. The mantle is about 2, 900 kilometers (1, 802 miles) thick, and makes up a whopping 84 percent of Earth’s total volume.

Concept note-2: -The Mantle: The mantle is the thickest layer of the Earth, making up about 82% of its volume.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: The thickest interior layer of the Earth is the mantle, which is about 1800 miles thick. The layer called the outer core is about 1400 miles thick. The thinnest interior layer of the Earth is the inner core, which is just about 800 miles thick.

Concept note-4: -Mantle-2, 900 km thick. Outer Core-2, 200 km thick. Inner Core-1, 230 to 1, 530 km thick.
