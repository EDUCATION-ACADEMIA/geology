A wide volcano with gentle slopes that has been formed by quiet, runny lava flows.

(A) Composite Volcano

(B) ** Shield Volcano

(C) Fissure Volcano

(D) Cinder-Cone Volcano

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A shield volcano is a broad volcano with sloping sides that is formed mainly out of runny lava that flows out of its central summit vent.

Concept note-2: -Shield volcanoes Where a volcano produces low viscosity, runny lava, it spreads far from the source and forms a volcano with gentle slopes: a shield volcano.

Concept note-3: -Cinder cones are the most common type of volcano. A cinder cone has a cone shape, but is much smaller than a composite volcano. Cinder cones rarely reach 300 meters in height but they have steep sides.

Concept note-4: -Shield volcanoes, the third type of volcano, are built almost entirely of fluid lava flows. Flow after flow pours out in all directions from a central summit vent, or group of vents, building a broad, gently sloping cone of flat, domical shape, with a profile much like that of a warriorâ€™s shield.

Concept note-5: -Pahoehoe (Pa-Hoy-Hoy) lava flows are very hot, thin and runny. When it cools is has a smooth to ropey texture because of the low silica content which makes it cool quickly. Pahoehoe flows creep along generally at less than 3 feet per minute but some flows have been measured at over 20 miles per hour.
