EARTH SCIENCE

TECTONICS

[SOURCES]
Earth’s plates are ____

(A) ** constantly moving and changing the face of the earth

(B) Never moves

(C) Moves seldom and very fast

(D) Moves when it feels like it

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The heat from radioactive processes within the planet’s interior causes the plates to move, sometimes toward and sometimes away from each other. This movement is called plate motion, or tectonic shift.

Concept note-2: -Even though plates move very slowly, their motion, called plate tectonics, has a huge impact on our planet. Plate tectonics form the oceans, continents, and mountains.

Concept note-3: -Plate motions cause mountains to rise where plates push together, or converge, and continents to fracture and oceans to form where plates pull apart, or diverge. The continents are embedded in the plates and drift passively with them, which over millions of years results in significant changes in Earth’s geography.

Concept note-4: -The tectonic plates slowly and constantly move but in many different directions. Some are moving toward each other, some are moving apart, and some are grinding past each other. Tectonic plate boundaries are grouped into three main types based on the different movements.

Concept note-5: -Plate tectonics is a scientific theory that explains how major landforms are created as a result of Earth’s subterranean movements. The theory, which solidified in the 1960s, transformed the earth sciences by explaining many phenomena, including mountain building events, volcanoes, and earthquakes.
