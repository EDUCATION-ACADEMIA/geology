The movement of tectonic plates can produce ____

(A) glaciers

(B) ** earthquakes

(C) impact craters

(D) fossils

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The tectonic plates are always slowly moving, but they get stuck at their edges due to friction. When the stress on the edge overcomes the friction, there is an earthquake that releases energy in waves that travel through the earth’s crust and cause the shaking that we feel.

Concept note-2: -Tectonic plates of the Earth are in constant motion but when they try to move past each other, they may get stuck due to rough surfaces on them and release a huge amount of energy which is radiated outwards from the fault in the form of seismic waves and cause an earthquake.

Concept note-3: -Eventually, the locked section succumbs to the pressure, and the plates move past each other rapidly. This movement causes a tectonic earthquake. The waves of released energy move through the Earth’s crust and cause the shaking we feel at an earthquake site.

Concept note-4: -The heat from radioactive processes within the planet’s interior causes the plates to move, sometimes toward and sometimes away from each other. This movement is called plate motion, or tectonic shift.

Concept note-5: -While most earthquakes are caused by the slipping of tectonic plates, minor earthquakes can also be caused by volcanic eruptions, collapse of rock formations on Earth’s surface, or underground explosions.
