Scientists think that the movement of Earth’s tectonic plates are caused by convection currents, that are located in the ____ ?

(A) outer core

(B) inner core

(C) ** mantle

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Magma is the molten rock below the crust, in the mantle. Tremendous heat and pressure within the earth cause the hot magma to flow in convection currents. These currents cause the movement of the tectonic plates that make up the earth’s crust.

Concept note-2: -Convection currents drive the movement of Earth’s rigid tectonic plates in the planet’s fluid molten mantle. In places where convection currents rise up towards the crust’s surface, tectonic plates move away from each other in a process known as seafloor spreading (Fig.

Concept note-3: -The heat from radioactive processes within the planet’s interior causes the plates to move, sometimes toward and sometimes away from each other. This movement is called plate motion, or tectonic shift.

Concept note-4: -The movement of these tectonic plates is likely caused by convection currents in the molten rock in Earth’s mantle below the crust.

Concept note-5: -Heat from the core and the mantle itself causes convection currents in the mantle. Hot columns of mantle material rise slowly. At the top of the asthenosphere, the hot material spreads out and pushes the cooler material out of the way. This cooler material sinks back into the mantle.
