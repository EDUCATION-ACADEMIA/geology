What types of ocean life is found near mid-ocean ridge

(A) ** a few types of fish that can withstand the pressure

(B) nothing

(C) sharks and dolfins

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Over 300 endemic species have been found near the vents, including corals, clams, shrimps, crabs and the now famous giant, red-tipped tubeworms, 4 m tall creatures that flourish in waters close to the hot springs.

Concept note-2: -True residents live their entire life in the open ocean. Only a few species are true residents, such as tuna, billfish, flying fish, sauries, pilotfish, remoras, dolphinfish, ocean sharks, and ocean sunfish. Most of these species migrate back and forth across open oceans, rarely venturing over continental shelves.

Concept note-3: -Without gas-filled spaces like lungs or swim bladders, organisms in the great deep are less affected by pressure than we imagine. Some ocean species perform vertical migrations of 1, 000 meters each day, experiencing a 100-atmosphere range of pressures with no harmful effects.
