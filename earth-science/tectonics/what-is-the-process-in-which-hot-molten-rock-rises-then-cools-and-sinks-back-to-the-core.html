What is the process in which hot molten rock rises, then cools and sinks back to the core?

(A) plate tectonics

(B) continental drift

(C) ** convection

(D) divergence

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Rock/magma gets more dense and rises. It then cools down and sinks.

Concept note-2: -In conduction, heat is transferred as atoms collide. The rising and sinking of warm and cooler material is called convection. Conduction brings hot material up toward the surface. It brings cooler material down toward the core, and so on.

Concept note-3: -Convection currents are the result of differential heating. Lighter (less dense), warm material rises while heavier (more dense) cool material sinks. It is this movement that creates circulation patterns known as convection currents in the atmosphere, in water, and in the mantle of Earth.

Concept note-4: -Convection currents, that occur within the molten rock in the mantle, act like a conveyor belt for the plates. Tectonic plates move in different directions. The direction of movement and type of plate margin is determined by which way the convection currents are flowing.

Concept note-5: -Geologists have hypothesized that the movement of tectonic plates is related to convection currents in the earthâ€™s mantle. Convection currents describe the rising, spread, and sinking of gas, liquid, or molten material caused by the application of heat.
