Which statement best explains how the continents have changed over the last 200 million years, according to the continental drift hypothesis?

(A) They drifted into and out of about fifteen different supercontinent configurations.

(B) They formed a stable supercontinent until the last ice age, when they rapidly moved to their current positions.

(C) ** They split apart from a supercontinent and very slowly moved to their current positions.

(D) They drifted into and out of about seven different supercontinent configurations.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Which statement best explains how the continents have changed over the last 200 million years, according to the continental drift hypothesis? They split apart from a supercontinent and very slowly moved to their current positions.

Concept note-2: -About 200 million years ago, all the continents on Earth were actually one huge “supercontinent” surrounded by one enormous ocean. This gigantic continent, called Pangaea, slowly broke apart and spread out to form the continents we know today. All Earth’s continents were once combined in one supercontinent, Pangaea.

Concept note-3: -Plate tectonics is the theory that Earth’s land masses are in constant motion. The realization that Earth’s land masses move was first proposed by Alfred Wegener, which he called continental drift.

Concept note-4: -Which statement describes one of Wegener’s ideas that contributed to the theory of continental drift? The continents were once connected as one landmass.
