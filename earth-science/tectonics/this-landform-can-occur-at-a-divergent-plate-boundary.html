This landform can occur at a divergent plate boundary.

(A) ocean trench

(B) ** mid-ocean ridge

(C) tall mountains

(D) transform fault

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Mid-ocean ridges occur along divergent plate boundaries, where new ocean floor is created as the Earth’s tectonic plates spread apart. As the plates separate, molten rock rises to the seafloor, producing enormous volcanic eruptions of basalt.

Concept note-2: -Two major landforms that are created from divergent boundaries are the rift valleys and mid-oceanic ridges. A divergent boundary occurs when two tectonic plates move away or spread apart from each other.

Concept note-3: -A divergent boundary occurs when two tectonic plates move away from each other. Along these boundaries, earthquakes are common and magma (molten rock) rises from the Earth’s mantle to the surface, solidifying to create new oceanic crust. The Mid-Atlantic Ridge is an example of divergent plate boundaries.

Concept note-4: -A divergent plate boundary often forms a mountain chain known as a ridge. This feature forms as magma escapes into the space between the spreading tectonic plates.

Concept note-5: -False, because the correct statement is: There are three types of divergent plate boundaries, namely continental-continental, oceanic-continental, and oceanic-oceanic.
