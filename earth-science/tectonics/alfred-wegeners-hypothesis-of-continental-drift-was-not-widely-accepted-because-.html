Alfred Wegener’s hypothesis of continental drift was not widely accepted because-

(A) he could not provide evidence to support his claim

(B) the puzzle fit was his only evidence and scientists thought it was a coincidence

(C) ** he could not explain what mechanism caused the plates to move

(D) he claimed continents drifted on the oceans

Concept note-1: -The main reason that Wegener’s hypothesis was not accepted was because he suggested no mechanism for moving the continents. He thought the force of Earth’s spin was sufficient to cause continents to move, but geologists knew that rocks are too strong for this to be true.
