New crust is always formed at the ____

(A) ** mid-ocean ridge

(B) deep-ocean trench

(C) asthenosphere

(D) Lithosphere

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The newest, thinnest crust on Earth is located near the center of mid-ocean ridge-the actual site of seafloor spreading. The age, density, and thickness of oceanic crust increases with distance from the mid-ocean ridge.

Concept note-2: -More than four cubic miles of ocean crust forms each year, constantly regenerating like new skin across the globe. This ocean crust arises along mid-ocean ridges-underwater mountain ranges that ripple along the ocean floor like jagged scars.

Concept note-3: -Oceanic crust is constantly formed at mid-ocean ridges, where tectonic plates are tearing apart from each other. As magma that wells up from these rifts in Earth’s surface cools, it becomes young oceanic crust. The age and density of oceanic crust increases with distance from mid-ocean ridges.

Concept note-4: -Mid-ocean ridges occur along divergent plate boundaries, where new ocean floor is created as the Earth’s tectonic plates spread apart. As the plates separate, molten rock rises to the seafloor, producing enormous volcanic eruptions of basalt.

Concept note-5: -Oceanic crust is formed as a result of decompression melting in the mantle at relatively shallow depths below the mid-ocean ridges, as the mantle rises in passive response to plate separation.
