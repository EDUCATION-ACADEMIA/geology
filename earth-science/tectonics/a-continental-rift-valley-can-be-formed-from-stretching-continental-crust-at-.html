A continental rift valley can be formed from stretching continental crust at ____

(A) transform plate boundaries

(B) convergent plate boundaries

(C) ** divergent plate boundaries

(D) all plate boundaries

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A divergent boundary may form beneath the continental lithosphere, where mantle convection cells cause the plates to push apart and form a Rift Valley.

Concept note-2: -Many rift valleys are part of “triple junctions, ” a type of divergent boundary where three tectonic plates meet at about 120° angles.

Concept note-3: -tectonic activity creates rift valleys The valleys form when continental crust is pulling apart allowing the land to drop down between parallel faults. These valleys also form when oceanic plates are moving apart forming a divergent boundary.

Concept note-4: -Rifting is defined as the splitting apart of a single tectonic plate into two or more tectonic plates separated by divergent plate boundaries. The rifting of a continental tectonic plate creates normal fault valleys, small tilted block mountains, and volcanism.

Concept note-5: -A divergent plate boundary on land rips apart continents (Figure below). When plate divergence occurs on land, the continental crust rifts, or splits. This effectively creates a new ocean basin as the pieces of the continent move apart.
