Which answer below correctly describes the formation of a volcanic island arc?

(A) Islands are created along the mid ocean ridge as new oceanic crust is formed.

(B) Islands are created one at a time as the plate moves across a hot spot in the mantle.

(C) ** Islands are created along a subduction zone as oceanic crust is forced back down in to the mantle.

(D) Islands are created as pressure is released when two tectonic plates are pulled apart.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Which answer below correctly describes the formation of a volcanic island arc? Islands are created along the mid ocean ridge as new oceanic crust is formed.

Concept note-2: -An island volcanic arc forms in an ocean basin via ocean-ocean subduction. The Aleutian Islands off the coast of Alaska and the Lesser Antilles south of Puerto Rico are examples. A continental volcanic arc forms along the margin of a continent where oceanic crust subducts beneath continental crust.

Concept note-3: -Volcanoes are one kind of feature that forms along convergent plate boundaries, where two tectonic plates collide and one moves beneath the other.

Concept note-4: -When two oceanic plates collide against each other, the older and therefore heavier of the two subducts beneath the other, initiating volcanic activity in a manner similar to that which occurs at an oceanic-continental convergent plate boundary and forming a volcanic island arc.

Concept note-5: -Island arcs are formed when tectonic plates collide at a subduction zone in the ocean. The oceanic plate sinks under the continental plate and lava leaks into the crust, bubbling up to eventually form islands.
