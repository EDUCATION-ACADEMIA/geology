What is the soft layer on which the tectonic plates move?

(A) lithosphere

(B) lower mantle

(C) ** asthensophere

(D) mesosphere

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The molten layer is located about 100 miles from the surface and is part of the asthenosphere, which sits under the Earth’s tectonic plates in the upper mantle. The asthenosphere is important for plate tectonics because it forms a relatively soft boundary that lets tectonic plates move through the mantle.

Concept note-2: -asthenosphere (convecting mantle) – the soft layer of the mantle on which the lithosphere floats.

Concept note-3: -The plates move on a hot flowing mantle layer called the asthenosphere, which is several hundred kilometers thick. Heat within the asthenosphere creates convection currents (similar to the currents that can be seen in the lava lamp).

Concept note-4: -Tremendous heat and pressure within the earth cause the hot magma to flow in convection currents. These currents cause the movement of the tectonic plates that make up the earth’s crust.

Concept note-5: -The asthenosphere is a “soft” layer beneath the hard lithosphere, and, as the name suggests, it is weak.
