TECTONICS

[SOURCES]
The shapes of continental coastlines were one of the main pieces of evidence that supported the hypothesis of

(A) seafloor spreading

(B) ridge push

(C) slab pull

(D) ** plate tectonics

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The four pieces of evidence for the continental drift include continents fitting together like a puzzle, scattering ancient fossils, rocks, mountain ranges, and the old climatic zones’ locations.

Concept note-2: -Evidence for Tectonic Plates Earthquakes, mountain building and volcanic activity occur mostly at the boundaries of the moving plates. Only shallow earthquakes occur where plates diverge at mid-ocean ridges, whereas earthquakes extend to great depth where plates converge at subduction zones.

Concept note-3: -Coastline Matching The similarity of coastlines for different continents suggests that they may once have been connected. But the fact that they were separated by sometimes thousands of miles suggested continental drift or plate tectonics .

Concept note-4: -The coastlines of South America and West Africa seem to match up. Scientists have found that rocks on the east coast of South America are the same as rocks found on the west coast of Africa. These rocks are different from rocks found in other places on Earth, suggesting that the continents were once connected.
