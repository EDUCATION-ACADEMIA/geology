Created by a divergent boundary.

(A) Rocky Mountains

(B) Mariana Trench

(C) ** Mid Atlantic ridge

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Mid Atlantic Ridge, like other ocean ridge systems, has developed as a consequence of the divergent motion between the Eurasian and North American, and African and South American Plates.

Concept note-2: -The Mid-Atlantic Ridge is a divergent plate boundary line. The features at the ridge are the result of convection currents within Earth dragging the North American and Eurasian Plates apart from each other. New oceanic crust is created along the boundary by rising and solidifying magma.

Concept note-3: -A divergent boundary occurs when two tectonic plates move away from each other. Along these boundaries, earthquakes are common and magma (molten rock) rises from the Earthâ€™s mantle to the surface, solidifying to create new oceanic crust. The Mid-Atlantic Ridge is an example of divergent plate boundaries.

Concept note-4: -When two plates are moving away from each other, we call this a divergent plate boundary. Along these boundaries, magma rises from deep within the Earth and erupts to form new crust on the lithosphere. Most divergent plate boundaries are underwater and form submarine mountain ranges called oceanic spreading ridges.

Concept note-5: -Divergent boundaries in the middle of the ocean contribute to seafloor spreading. As plates made of oceanic crust pull apart, a crack in the ocean floor appears. Magma then oozes up from the mantle to fill in the space between the plates, forming a raised ridge called a mid-ocean ridge.
