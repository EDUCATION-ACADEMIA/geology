A geologist is digging a hole in order to get samples of the soil from different depths. At a certain depth she notices that water has begun to fill in the bottom of her hole. Based on this observation, she must have dug her hole below the

(A) watershed.

(B) thermocline.

(C) headwaters.

(D) ** water table.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The deepest penetration of the Earth’s surface is the Kola Superdeep Borehole in Russia at 40, 230 feet. Originally a Soviet project and a source of pride for the USSR, the aim was simply to drill as deep as possible into the Earth’s crust. The borehole is 9 inches in diameter.

Concept note-2: -Groundwater can also be extracted through a well drilled into the aquifer. A well is a pipe in the ground that fills with groundwater. This water can be brought to the surface by a pump. Shallow wells may go dry if the water table falls below the bottom of the well.

Concept note-3: -Essentially a seep well is a hole dug in the ground, near a water source that goes below the natural water table. The water from the water table seeps through the earth and into the hole that you dug.

Concept note-4: -The unrestrained exploitation of groundwater is perceived to cause the decrease in water level and drying up of older wells.
