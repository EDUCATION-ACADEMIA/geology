unit cell may contain

(A) ** atoms, molecules and ions

(B) only atoms

(C) only molecules

(D) only ions

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The smallest repeating unit of a crystal lattice is the unit cell. The simple cubic unit cell contains only eight atoms, molecules, or ions at the corners of a cube. A body-centered cubic (bcc) unit cell contains one additional component in the center of the cube.

Concept note-2: -The unit cell consists of lattice points that represent the locations of atoms or ions. The entire structure then consists of this unit cell repeating in three dimensions, as illustrated in Figure 4.1. 1.

Concept note-3: -Therefore, the total number of atoms present per unit cell = 2 atoms.

Concept note-4: -The characteristics of a unit cell are: (i) The dimensions are measured along the three edges, a, b and c. These edges can form different angles, they may be mutually perpendicular or may not. (ii) The angles held by the edges are (between b and c) (between a and c) and (between a and b).
