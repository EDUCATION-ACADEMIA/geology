Common type of chemical bonding in mineals

(A) Ionic bonding and covalent bonding

(B) ** metallic bonding and covalent bonding

(C) Covalent bonding and vander walls bonding

(D) Metallic bonding and ionic bonding

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Chemical bonds in minerals are of four types: covalent, ionic, metallic, or Van der Waals, with covalent and ionic bonds most common.

Concept note-2: -Most minerals are characterized by ionic or covalent bonds, or a combination of the two, but one other type of bond which is geologically important is the metallic bond. Elements that behave as metals have outer electrons that are relatively loosely held.

Concept note-3: -Most minerals are characterized by ionic bonds, covalent bonds, or a combination of the two, but there are other types of bonds that are important in minerals, including metallic bonds and weaker electrostatic forces (hydrogen or Van der Waals bonds).

Concept note-4: -Bonding in most of the minerals we geologists deal with is largely ionic, however, covalent bonding does occur to a lesser degree. 3.
