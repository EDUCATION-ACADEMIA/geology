Which of the following tools would you use to test a mineral’s volume?

(A) ** graduated cylinder

(B) scale

(C) ceramic streak plate

(D) glass tile

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Liquid volume is usually measured using either a graduated cylinder or a buret. As the name implies, a graduated cylinder is a cylindrical glass or plastic tube sealed at one end, with a calibrated scale etched (or marked) on the outside wall.

Concept note-2: -A graduated cylinder / cylinder measuring / mixing cylinder is a piece of laboratory apparatus used to measure the volume of a liquids, chemicals or solutions. Graduated cylinders are more accurate and precise than laboratory flasks and beakers.

Concept note-3: -Among the following, graduated cylinder is the best suited for accurately measuring the volume of a liquid. Using graduated cylinder reduces the error in the measurement of volume of liquid.

Concept note-4: -Instruments for Measuring Volume. Glassware used in lab settings come in a wide variety of sizes and shapes. Erlenmeyer Flasks. Florence Flasks. Beakers. Graduated Cylinders. Volumetric Flasks. Burettes. Pipettes. More items •11-Nov-2021
