It is the measure or level of ground shaking that correlates more directly with hazard impact.

(A) SEISMOGRAPH

(B) ** INTENSITY

(C) MAGNITUDE

(D) BAROMETER

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Seismographs are instruments used to record the motion of the ground during an earthquake. They are installed in the ground throughout the world and operated as part of a seismographic network.

Concept note-2: -The Modified Mercalli (MM) Intensity Scale is used in the United States. Based on Giuseppe Mercalli’s Mercalli intensity scale of 1902, the modified 1931 scale is composed of increasing levels of intensity that range from observable quake impacts from light shaking to catastrophic destruction.

Concept note-3: -Two important local geologic factors that affect the level of shaking experienced in earthquakes are (1) the softness of the surface rocks and (2) the thickness of surface sediments.

Concept note-4: -Although numerousintensity scales have been developed over the last several hundred years to evaluate the effects of earthquakes, the one currently used in the United States is the Modified Mercalli (MM) Intensity Scale. It was developed in 1931 by the American seismologists Harry Wood and Frank Neumann.
