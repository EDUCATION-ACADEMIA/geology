There is a break in the earth’s crust under Lake Erie. What do geologists call this break?

(A) tsunami

(B) ** fault

(C) earthquake

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Faults are cracks in the earth’s crust along which there is movement. These can be massive (the boundaries between the tectonic plates themselves) or very small. If tension builds up along a fault and then is suddenly released, the result is an earthquake.

Concept note-2: -A fault is a fracture or zone of fractures between two blocks of rock. Faults allow the blocks to move relative to each other. This movement may occur rapidly, in the form of an earthquake-or may occur slowly, in the form of creep.

Concept note-3: -A fracture in the earth crusts is called fault. Faults are fractures in Earth’s crust where rocks on either side of the crack have slid past each other.

Concept note-4: -It seems that within the field of fracture mechanics, some authors use “fracture” to refer to the mechanism of creating new surfaces within a body by breaking the material bonds and reserve the word “crack” for the sharp-tipped discontinuity that results from fracture of a brittle material.
