Which wave can travel through all materials?

(A) ** P-primary

(B) S-secondary

(C) L-Long

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -P waves are the fastest seismic waves and can move through solid, liquid, or gas.

Concept note-2: -P waves can travel through liquid and solids and gases, while S waves only travel through solids. Scientists use this information to help them determine the structure of Earth.

Concept note-3: -Electromagnetic waves are not like sound waves because they do not need molecules to travel. This means that electromagnetic waves can travel through air, solid objects and even space.

Concept note-4: -Primary waves, also known as P waves or pressure waves, are longitudinal compression waves similar to the motion of a slinky (SF Fig. 7.1 A). Secondary waves, or S waves, are slower than P waves.

Concept note-5: -P waves can move through solid rock and fluids, like water or the liquid layers of the Earth. It squishes and stretches the rock it moves through just like sound waves compress and expand the air as they move through it.
