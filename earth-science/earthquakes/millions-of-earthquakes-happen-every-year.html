EARTHQUAKES

[SOURCES]
Millions of earthquakes happen every year.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The National Earthquake Information Center now locates about 20, 000 earthquakes around the globe each year, or approximately 55 per day.

Concept note-2: -The National Earthquake Information Center (U.S.) reports 12, 000-14, 000 earthquakes a year around the world, or 35 a day. Throughout the world, there are one “great” (magnitude 8.0 or more), 18 “major” (7.0-7.9), 120 “large” (6.0-6.9) and 1, 000 “moderate” (5.0-5.9) earthquakes in an average year.

Concept note-3: -Earthquakes happen every day all over the world, along both tectonic plate edges and interiors. Earthquakes occur along faults, which are fractures between blocks of rock that allow the blocks to move relative to one another.

Concept note-4: -It is estimated that there are 500, 000 detectable earthquakes in the world each year. 100, 000 of those can be felt, and 100 of them cause damage.
