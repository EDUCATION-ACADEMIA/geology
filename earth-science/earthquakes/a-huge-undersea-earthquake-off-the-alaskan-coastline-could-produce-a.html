A huge undersea earthquake off the Alaskan coastline could produce a

(A) ** tsunami

(B) cyclone

(C) hurricane

(D) thunderstorm

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When a great earthquake ruptures, the faulting can cause vertical slip that is large enough to disturb the overlying ocean, thus generating a tsunami that will travel outwards in all directions.

Concept note-2: -Tsunami waves are caused by disturbance under the sea or ocean bed.

Concept note-3: -Sometimes earthquakes occur on faults under the ocean. If an earthquake disturbs the sea floor by shaking it up and down, then the earthquake energy can contribute to large waves at nearby coastlines, called a tsunami.

Concept note-4: -If big enough and close enough to the ocean floor, the energy from such an earthquake can cause the ocean floor to suddenly rise (uplift) or fall (subside). It is this sudden vertical displacement of the ocean floor that typically sets a tsunami in motion.
