A measure of the strength of an earthquake is called the ____

(A) focus

(B) ** magnitude

(C) epicenter

(D) intensity

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Magnitude is the most common measure of an earthquakeâ€™s size. It is a measure of the size of the earthquake source and is the same number no matter where you are or what the shaking feels like.

Concept note-2: -The Richter Scale It used a formula based on the amplitude of the largest wave recorded on a specific type of seismometer and the distance between the earthquake and the seismometer.

Concept note-3: -There are two ways by which we can measure the strength of an earthquake: magnitude and intensity. Magnitude is proportional to the energy released by an earthquake at the focus. It is calculated from earthquakes recorded by an instrument called seismograph. It is represented by Arabic Numbers (e.g. 4.8, 9.0).

Concept note-4: -Richter scale is used to measure the magnitude of the earthquakes. The Richter scale has numbers from 1 to 10.

Concept note-5: -Magnitude measures the energy released at the source of the earthquake. Magnitude is determined from measurements on seismographs. Intensity measures the strength of shaking produced by the earthquake at a certain location. Intensity is determined from effects on people, human structures, and the natural environment.
