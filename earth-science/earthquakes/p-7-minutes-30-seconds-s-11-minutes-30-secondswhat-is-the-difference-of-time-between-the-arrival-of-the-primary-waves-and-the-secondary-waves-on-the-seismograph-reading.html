P= 7 minutes 30 seconds S= 11 minutes 30 secondsWhat is the difference of time between the arrival of the primary waves and the secondary waves on the seismograph reading?

(A) 7 minutes 20 seconds

(B) 11 minutes 20 seconds

(C) ** 4 minutes

(D) 5 minutes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There are two types of seismic waves, primary waves and secondary waves. Primary waves, also known as P waves or pressure waves, are longitudinal compression waves similar to the motion of a slinky (SF Fig. 7.1 A). Secondary waves, or S waves, are slower than P waves.

Concept note-2: -With increasing distance from the earthquake the time difference between the arrival of the P waves and the arrival of the S waves increases.

Concept note-3: -The P waves are easy to identify because they are always first to arrive and their amplitude is smaller than the S wave. S waves are indicated by an abrupt change in wave amplitude. In the seismogram below, we see that the S wave arrived at about 34 sec after the P wave arrived.

Concept note-4: -P and S waves from an earthquake travel at different speeds, and this difference helps locate the earthquake “epicenter” (where the disturbance took place).
