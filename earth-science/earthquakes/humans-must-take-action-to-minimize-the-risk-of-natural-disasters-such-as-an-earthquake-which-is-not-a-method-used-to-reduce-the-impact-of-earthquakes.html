Humans must take action to minimize the risk of natural disasters such as an earthquake. Which is NOT a method used to reduce the impact of earthquakes?

(A) reinforcing existing infrastructure

(B) planning the location of infrastructure

(C) ** reducing the restrictions of building codes

(D) designing earthquake-resistant infrastructure

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -We cannot prevent natural earthquakes from occurring but we can significantly mitigate their effects by identifying hazards, building safer structures, and providing education on earthquake safety. By preparing for natural earthquakes we can also reduce the risk from human induced earthquakes.

Concept note-2: -Awareness, education, preparedness, and prediction and warning systems can reduce the disruptive impacts of a natural disaster on communities. Mitigation measures such as adoption of zoning, land-use practices, and building codes are needed, however, to prevent or reduce actual damage from hazards.

Concept note-3: -Do not attempt to walk, swim, or drive through the floods. Stay clear of bridges over fast-moving water. Keep an eye out for evacuation alerts. Move to higher ground. More items •12-Aug-2020

Concept note-4: -Doorways. Doors may slam shut and cause injuries. Windows, bookcases, tall furniture and light fixtures. You could be hurt by shattered glass or heavy objects. Elevators. Downed power lines-stay at least 10 metres away to avoid injury. Coastline.
