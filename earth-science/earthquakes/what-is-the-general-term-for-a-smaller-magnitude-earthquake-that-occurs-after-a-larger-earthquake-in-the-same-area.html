What is the general term for a smaller magnitude earthquake that occurs after a larger earthquake in the same area?

(A) ** Aftershock

(B) Foreshock

(C) Seismic Gap

(D) Tsunami

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -aftershock, any of several lower-magnitude earthquakes that follow the main shock of a larger earthquake.

Concept note-2: -Aftershocks are smaller earthquakes that occur in the same general area during the days to years following a larger event or “mainshock.” They occur within 1-2 fault lengths away and during the period of time before the background seismicity level has resumed.

Concept note-3: -Aftershocks can continue over a period of weeks, months, or years. In general, the larger the mainshock, the larger and more numerous the aftershocks, and the longer they will continue. Foreshocks are relatively smaller earthquakes that precede the largest earthquake in a series, which is termed the mainshock.

Concept note-4: -This is known as a seismogram. The bigger the quake the larger the size or amplitude of the waves recorded. In general, a magnitude 3 earthquake is about the smallest that one can feel.

Concept note-5: -Aftershocks are a sequence of earthquakes that happen after a larger mainshock on a fault.
