What is the point beneath the Earth’s crust where rocks first break under stress causing an earthquake?

(A) First seismic surface wave

(B) Epicenter

(C) ** Focus or hypocenter

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The location below the earth’s surface where the earthquake starts is called the hypocenter, and the location directly above it on the surface of the earth is called the epicenter.

Concept note-2: -The spot underground where the rock first breaks is called the focus, or hypocenter of the earthquake. The place right above the focus (at the ground surface) is called the epicenter of the earthquake.

Concept note-3: -Focus (aka Hypocenter) is the location in the Earth where the earthquake starts.

Concept note-4: -The focus is point inside the earth where the earthquake started, sometimes called the hypocenter, and the point on the surface of the earth directly above the focus is called the epicenter.

Concept note-5: -The focus or hypocenter is the point beneath the Earth’s surface where the crust or mantle first ruptures to cause an earthquake (Figure 3-4). The depth of the earthquake below the surface is called its focal depth.
