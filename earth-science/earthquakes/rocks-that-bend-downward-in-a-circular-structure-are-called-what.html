rocks that bend downward in a circular structure are called what?

(A) dome

(B) monocline

(C) ** basin

(D) anticline

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A syncline is a fold that bends downward, causing the youngest rocks are to be at the center and the oldest are on the outside. When rocks bend downward in a circular structure, that structure is called abasin.

Concept note-2: -A basin is a circular fold that is concave upward-i.e., the strata dip inward toward a central area.

Concept note-3: -An upward fold is called an anticline, while a downward fold is called a syncline. In many areas it’s common to find a series of anticlines and synclines (as in Figure 12.5), although some sequences of rocks are folded into a single anticline or syncline.

Concept note-4: -Folding occurs when the Earth’s crust bends away from a flat surface. A bend upward results in an anticline and a bend downward results in a syncline.

Concept note-5: -Figure 10.6: Rocks that were originally deposited in horizontal layers can subsequently deform by tectonic forces into folds and faults. Folds constitute the twists and bends in rocks. Faults are planes of detachment resulting when rocks on either side of the displacement slip past one another.
