What does hazard mean?

(A) reductions in the levels of something

(B) ** sources of danger

(C) weak points in a structure of something

(D) thoughts that cause feelings of fear

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A hazard is a source or a situation with the potential for harm in terms of human injury or ill-health, damage to property, damage to the environment, or a combination of these.

Concept note-2: -A hazard is any source of potential damage, harm or adverse health effects on something or someone. Basically, a hazard is the potential for harm or an adverse effect (for example, to people as health effects, to organizations as property or equipment losses, or to the environment).

Concept note-3: -Hazard sources A hazard source is a location or condition that can give rise to a hazard. Hazard sources include, but are not limited to: areas of low flow or stagnant water within the facility distribution system (dead legs and disused taps)

Concept note-4: -A hazard is any source of potential damage, harm or adverse health effects on something or someone. Basically, a hazard is the potential for harm or an adverse effect (for example, to people as health effects, to organizations as property or equipment losses, or to the environment).

Concept note-5: -Inhalation, ingestion and skin absorption of material hazardous to health (including aerosols and particulates) Use of flammable and explosive materials. Lack of oxygen (asphyxia) Presence of corrosive substances. Reactive/unstable substances. Presence of sensitizers.
