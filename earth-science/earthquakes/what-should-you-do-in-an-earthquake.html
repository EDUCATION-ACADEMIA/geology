EARTHQUAKES

[SOURCES]
What should you do in an earthquake?

(A) Stay indoors

(B) ** Go outside as fast as possible

(C) Walk near glass doors & breakable structures

(D) Stay wherever you are

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Do not move from where you are. However, move away from buildings, trees, streetlights, and utility wires. If you are in open space, stay there until the shaking stops. The greatest danger exists directly outside buildings; at exits; and alongside exterior walls.

Concept note-2: -If you are outside, stay outside. Go to an open area away from trees, telephone poles, and buildings. Once in the open, get down low and stay there until the shaking stops. The area near the outside walls of a building is the most dangerous place to be.

Concept note-3: -Stay inside. Drop under heavy furniture such as a table, desk, bed or any solid furniture. Cover your head and torso to prevent being hit by falling objects. Hold onto the object that you are under so that you remain covered. More items

Concept note-4: -The Seven Steps to Earthquake Safety. Secure Your Space. Plan to be Safe. Organize Disaster Supplies. Minimize Financial Hardship. Drop, Cover, and Hold On. Improve Safety. Reconnect and Restore. More items
