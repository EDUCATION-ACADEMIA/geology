True or False? When you are out in the ocean, you might not even be able to detect a tsunami wave.

(A) ** True

(B) False

(C) Wait, what?

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The crests of tsunami waves may be more than a hundred kilometers or more away from each other. Therefore, passengers on boats at sea, far away from shore where the water is deep, will not feel nor see the tsunami waves as they pass by underneath at high speeds.

Concept note-2: -To detect and observe tsunamis as they move across the ocean, NOAA depends on networks of seismic and sea-level observation systems. These networks are owned and operated by a number of domestic and international organizations, including NOAA. The NOAA Tsunami Program runs the U.S. Tsunami Warning System.

Concept note-3: -A: Tsunamis are disasters that can be generated in all of the worldâ€™s oceans, inland seas, and in any large body of water. Each region of the world appears to have its own cycle of frequency and pattern in generating tsunamis that range in size from small to the large and highly destructive events.

Concept note-4: -Deep-ocean tsunami detection buoys are one of two types of instrument used by the Bureau of Meteorology (Bureau) to confirm the existence of tsunami waves generated by undersea earthquakes. These buoys observe and record changes in sea level out in the deep ocean.

Concept note-5: -A tsunami is a series of extremely long waves caused by a large and sudden displacement of the ocean, usually the result of an earthquake below or near the ocean floor.
