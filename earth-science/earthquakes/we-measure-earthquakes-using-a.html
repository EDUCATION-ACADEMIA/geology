[SOURCES]
We measure earthquakes using a:

(A) Weighing scale

(B) ** Seismograph

(C) Pendulum

(D) Digital thermometer

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A seismograph is the primary earthquake measuring instrument. The seismograph produces a digital graphic recording of the ground motion caused by the seismic waves . The digital recording is called a seismogram. A network of worldwide seismographs detects and measures the strength and duration of the earthquakeâ€™s waves.

Concept note-2: -Seismographs are instruments used to record the motion of the ground during an earthquake. They are installed in the ground throughout the world and operated as part of a seismographic network.

Concept note-3: -The first widely-used method, the Richter scale, was developed by Charles F. Richter in 1934. It used a formula based on the amplitude of the largest wave recorded on a specific type of seismometer and the distance between the earthquake and the seismometer.

Concept note-4: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is Earth Quake. A seismograph records the waves reaching the surface during an earthquake.

Concept note-5: -Seismograph is the instrument that records the tremors of an earthquake. This instrument also helps seismologists to map the interior of the Earth.
