Rank the seismic waves in order of appearance when an earthquake first occurs as measured by a seismograph.

(A) S waves, P waves, then surface waves

(B) ** P waves, S waves, then surface waves

(C) S and P waves at the same time, then surface waves

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When an earthquake occurs the P and S waves travel outward from the region of the fault that ruptured and the P waves arrive at the seismometer first, followed by the S-wave.

Concept note-2: -The P wave will be the first wiggle that is bigger than the background signals). Because P waves are the fastest seismic waves, they will usually be the first ones that your seismograph records. The next set of seismic waves on your seismogram will be the S waves. These are usually bigger than the P waves.

Concept note-3: -P Waves. The first kind of body wave is the P wave or primary wave. Itâ€™s the fastest kind of seismic wave, and the first to arrive at a seismic station. P waves can move through solid rock and fluids, like water or the liquid layers of the Earth.

Concept note-4: -There are two types of body waves: P-waves travel fastest and through solids, liquids, and gases; S-waves only travel through solids. Surface waves are the slowest, but they do the most damage in an earthquake.

Concept note-5: -Body Waves-Primary (P) & Secondary (S) Waves. Surface Waves-Rayleigh & Love Waves.
