What are shadow zones

(A) ** a place where no seismic waves can be detected from a specific earthquake

(B) a place on earth where shadow appear larger

(C) the place where shadow of an earthquake appear

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The seismic shadow zone is the rea of the Earth’s surface where seismographs cannot detect an earthquake after the waves have passed through the earth. P waves are refracted by the liquid outer core and are not detected between 104° and 140°

Concept note-2: -A seismic shadow zone is an area of the Earth’s surface where seismographs cannot detect direct P waves and/or S waves from an earthquake. This is due to liquid layers or structures within the Earth’s surface.

Concept note-3: -1. Seismic shadow zone is the zone on Earth’s surface where seismographs don’t detect earthquakes because seismic waves don’t reach there. 2. The P-wave shadow zone does not receive any direct P waves as it gets refracted.

Concept note-4: -A seismic shadow zone is not in a constant place; each earthquake epicenter has a different shadow zone. The finding of shadow zones allowed scientists to prove Earth had an interior layer of liquid.

Concept note-5: -A zone between 105° and 145° from the epicentre was recognized as the shadow zone for both the wave types. The entire zone beyond 105° does not receive S-waves.
