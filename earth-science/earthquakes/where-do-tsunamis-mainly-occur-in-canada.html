Where do tsunamis mainly occur in Canada?

(A) East coast of Canada (The maritime provinces)

(B) ** West Coast of Canada (Vancouver/Victoria, British Columbia)

(C) The Arctic ocean (Nunavut)

(D) The Great Lakes of Canada

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The west coast of Canada is particularly vulnerable to earthquake-induced tsunamis due to the presence of both nearby and distant subduction source zones at the margin of the Pacific basin.

Concept note-2: -The coast of British Columbia is at the highest risk of tsunamis in Canada but tsunamis can occur on any coastal area.

Concept note-3: -A study by the SFU Centre for Natural Hazard Research found that North and West Vancouver are at low risk for a tsunami.

Concept note-4: -Victoria is at risk of experiencing tsunami hazards from a number of potential sources including the Cascadia Subduction Zone, the Alaska-Aleutian Subduction Zone and local shallow crustal faults.

Concept note-5: -While rare, a tsunami along BC’s coast is a real threat. British Columbia’s coast is a geologically active area, where two massive tectonic plates come together.
