[SOURCES]
The center of a bar magnet is the strongest

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The two ends of the bar magnet exert the maximum magnetic force. It is equally strong at the north pole and the south pole. The force exerted by the middle of the magnet is weaker.

Concept note-2: -It is strongest at the poles. So, what are magnetic poles? Magnetic poles are opposite ends of a magnet where the magnetic field is strongest.

Concept note-3: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is zero. Magnetism at the centre of Bar magnet is zero.

Concept note-4: -Lines of force are three-dimensional, surrounding a bar magnet on all sides and it is crowded at pole and parallel in middle making magnetic field weaker in middle.
