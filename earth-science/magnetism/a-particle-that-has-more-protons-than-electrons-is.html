A particle that has more protons than electrons is

(A) ** positively charged

(B) negatively charged

(C) neutral

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If it has more protons than electrons, it is a positive ion.

Concept note-2: -If an object has more protons than electrons, then the net charge on the object is positive. If there are more electrons than protons, then the net charge on the object is negative. If there are equal numbers of protons and electrons, then the object is electrically neutral.

Concept note-4: -A cation is an ion with positive charge, which means it has more protons (positively-charged particles) than electrons (negatively-charged particles).
