A butte is ____

(A) a curved rock formation, formed by a combination of erosion forces

(B) ** an isolated hill with steep, even sides, and a flat top

(C) the center of the earth

(D) the thin, rocky, outermost layer of the Earth

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Buttes are smaller flat topped mountains or hills with steep slopes on all sides. They are typically topped by a hard cap rock that is resistant to erosion protects the softer lower layers beneath.

Concept note-2: -A mesa is a flat-topped mountain or hill. It is a wide, flat, elevated landform with steep sides. Mesa is a Spanish word that means table. Spanish explorers of the American southwest, where many mesas are found, used the word because the tops of mesas look like the tops of tables.

Concept note-3: -Buttes are tall, flat-topped, steep-sided towers of rock. Buttes were created through the process of erosion, the gradual wearing away of earth by water, wind, and ice. Buttes were once part of flat, elevated areas of land known as mesas or plateaus.

Concept note-4: -In geomorphology, a butte (/bjuÀêt/) is an isolated hill with steep, often vertical sides and a small, relatively flat top; buttes are smaller landforms than mesas, plateaus, and tablelands.
