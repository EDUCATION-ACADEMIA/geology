What does the switch do in a circuit?

(A) It supplies the power.

(B) It carries electricity.

(C) ** It opens and closes the circuit.

(D) It is an insulator.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An electric switch is an electrical component that breaks or closes an electric circuit. Opening the switch (breaking the circuit) means turning off the electrical device and closing the switch (completing the circuit) allows an electrical current to flow so that the device is on.

Concept note-2: -The terms “open” and “closed” refer to switches as well as entire circuits. An open switch is one without continuity: current cannot flow through it. A closed switch is one that provides a direct (low resistance) path for current to flow through.

Concept note-3: -Electric current flows only in those circuits which are in a closed loop. When the switch is in open position, the circuit becomes incomplete and hence, current will not flow in the circuit.

Concept note-4: -The function of switch in an electric circuit is to either make or break the electric circuit. A switch is used to turn current to an electrical appliance either on or off.

Concept note-5: -A device designed to open or close a circuit under controlled conditions is called a switch.
