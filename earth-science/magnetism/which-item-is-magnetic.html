(B) paper

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Steel is an iron-based alloy. Most forms of steel, including stainless steel, are magnetic.

Concept note-2: -Iron is an extremely well-known ferromagnetic metal. It is, in fact, the strongest ferromagnetic metal. It forms an integral part of the earthâ€™s core and imparts its magnetic properties to our planet. That is why the Earth acts as a permanent magnet on its own.

Concept note-3: -Iron, Nickel, and Cobalt are the magnetic substances as objects made up of these materials are attracted by a magnet. Also, magnetic materials can be magnetized or we can say that magnetic materials can be converted into magnets.

Concept note-4: -Examples of magnetic substances include iron, nickel, cobalt, stainless steel, and many rare earth metals. Diamagnetic materials like copper and gold are weakly repelled by a magnetic field. Paramagnetic materials like calcium and aluminum are weakly attracted by a magnetic field.
