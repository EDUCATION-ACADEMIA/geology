A step-up transformer is a transformer that increases

(A) current

(B) ** voltage

(C) change

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A transformer that increases the voltage from primary to secondary (more secondary winding turns than primary winding turns) is called a step-up transformer. Conversely, a transformer designed to do just the opposite is called a step-down transformer.

Concept note-2: -Step-up transformers increase the voltage from the input to the output of the transformer. In this configuration, the primary winding features fewer turnings than the secondary winding. This greater number of windings produces a higher voltage in the secondary side, increasing the output voltage.

Concept note-3: -A step-up transformer increases the AC voltage.
