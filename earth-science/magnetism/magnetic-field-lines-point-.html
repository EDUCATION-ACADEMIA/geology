MAGNETISM

[SOURCES]
Magnetic field lines point ____

(A) ** north to south

(B) south to north

(C) from end to middle

(D) from middle to end

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Magnetic field lines are the imaginary lines around a magnet which gives us the pattern of magnetic field of the magnet. These lines don’t start or end. They form a closed loop. They flow from North pole to South pole outside the magnet, while they flow from South pole to North pole inside the magnet.

Concept note-2: -Magnetic field lines outside of a permanent magnet always run from the north magnetic pole to the south magnetic pole. Therefore, the magnetic field lines of the earth run from the southern geographic hemisphere towards the northern geographic hemisphere.

Concept note-3: -The magnetic field is a zone where the force is active along imaginary lines. From the south magnetic pole to the north magnetic pole, this force has an effect on all magnetized objects, such as the needle of a compass. Under the effect of Earth’s magnetic field, the needle always points toward the north magnetic pole.

Concept note-4: -The magnetic dipole moment is a vector quantity with direction perpendicular to the current loop in the right-hand-rule direction. From south pole to North pole of a bar magnet. The direction of magnetic dipole moment is from south pole to north pole of a bar magnet.
