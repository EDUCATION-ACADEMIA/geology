Which powerline would most likely have the highest voltage?

(A) The one supplying a residential area.

(B) The one near a power distribution center.

(C) ** The one near a power plant.

(D) The one along a highway.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Record Bitter magnets The strongest continuous manmade magnetic field, 45 T, was produced by a hybrid device, consisting of a Bitter magnet inside a superconducting magnet. The resistive magnet produces 33.5 T and the superconducting coil produces the remaining 11.5 T.

Concept note-2: -The induced voltage can be increased by winding the wire into a coil because the field lines intersect the circuit multiple times. The direction of the magnetic field produced by a coil can be determined by the right hand grip rule.

Concept note-3: -The magnetic field around a magnet is the strongest at the poles.

Concept note-4: -The thickness of the wire affects the strength of the electromagnet because the resistance of the circuit is inversely proportional to the cross-sectional area. So if the current will drop, then more current will flow in the electromagnet. Thus, the thickness of the wire increases the strength of the electromagnet.
