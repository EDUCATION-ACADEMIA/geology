What is a device that generates electricity by combining certain chemicals?

(A) a charger

(B) a plug

(C) ** a battery

(D) a paperclip

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A fuel cell is a device that generates electricity by a chemical reaction. Every fuel cell has two electrodes called, respectively, the anode and cathode. The reactions that produce electricity take place at the electrodes.

Concept note-2: -A battery is a device that stores chemical energy and converts it to electrical energy. The chemical reactions in a battery involve the flow of electrons from one material (electrode) to another, through an external circuit. The flow of electrons provides an electric current that can be used to do work.

Concept note-3: -Batteries produce electricity A chemical reaction between the metals and the electrolyte frees more electrons in one metal than it does in the other. The metal that frees more electrons develops a positive charge, and the other metal develops a negative charge.

Concept note-4: -An electric generator is a device that converts a form of energy into electricity.

Concept note-5: -Hence, a device which converts electrical energy into mechanical energy is called a motor.
