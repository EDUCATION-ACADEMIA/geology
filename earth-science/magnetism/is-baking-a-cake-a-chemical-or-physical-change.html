Is baking a cake a chemical or physical change?

(A) chemical because you create a new substance

(B) chemical because it produces an odor

(C) chemical because it is irreversable

(D) ** All of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Once the cake is formed, we cannot get back the original batter and the chemical composition of cake is different from that of batter. In any physical change, the chemical composition of a substance does not change. Hence baking of cake is not a physical change but a chemical change.

Concept note-2: -When you bake a cake, the ingredients go through a chemical change. A chemical change occurs when the molecules that compose two or more substances are rearranged to form a new substance! When you start baking, you have a mixture of ingredients. The flour, egg, sugar, etc.

Concept note-3: -As you bake a cake, you are producing an endothermic chemical reaction that changes ooey-gooey batter into a fluffy, delicious treat!

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: a. Baking of cake is a chemical change.

Concept note-5: -Baking of cake is an example of Irreversible change.
