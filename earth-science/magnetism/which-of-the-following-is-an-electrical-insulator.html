Which of the following is an electrical insulator?

(A) copper

(B) ** rubber

(C) aluminum

(D) iron

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Rubber is an electrical insulator.

Concept note-2: -Rubber materials feature excellent electrical insulation capabilities, making them useful for preventing the dangerous effects caused by electricity transferring to other objects. Rubber slows down electrons, making it the most natural and efficient insulator in the world.

Concept note-3: -Some common insulator materials are glass, plastic, rubber, air, and wood. Insulators are used to protect us from the dangerous effects of electricity flowing through conductors.

Concept note-4: -Rubber is known to be an insulator because rubber can limit the transfer of electricity. The rubber properties prevent the electrons to be able to freely move and the addition of the electrons being tightly bounded makes rubber a good insulator .

Concept note-5: -Materials that do not let current flow easily are called insulators. Most nonmetal materials such as plastic, wood and rubber are insulators.
