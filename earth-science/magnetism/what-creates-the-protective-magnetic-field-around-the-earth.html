What creates the protective magnetic field around the Earth?

(A) ** Earth’s core

(B) Photons Sun

(C) The Moon

(D) Volcanoes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Generated by the motion of molten iron in Earth’s core, the magnetic field protects our planet from cosmic radiation and from the charged particles emitted by our Sun.

Concept note-2: -The Earth’s outer core is in a state of turbulent convection as the result of radioactive heating and chemical differentiation. This sets up a process that is a bit like a naturally occurring electrical generator, where the convective kinetic energy is converted to electrical and magnetic energy.

Concept note-3: -The Earth’s magnetic field is generated in the fluid outer core by a self-exciting dynamo process. Electrical currents flowing in the slowly moving molten iron generate the magnetic field.
