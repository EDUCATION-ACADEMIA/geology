The regions of a magnet that exert the strongest force are called ____

(A) magnetism

(B) ** magnetic poles

(C) magnetic domain

(D) magnetic field

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -magnetic pole, region at each end of a magnet where the external magnetic field is strongest. A bar magnet suspended in Earth’s magnetic field orients itself in a north–south direction. The north-seeking pole of such a magnet, or any similar pole, is called a north magnetic pole.

Concept note-2: -The magnetic field of a bar magnet is strongest at either pole of the magnet. It is equally strong at the north pole when compared with the south pole. The force is weaker in the middle of the magnet and halfway between the pole and the center.

Concept note-3: -One end is called the north pole, the other is called the south pole. If you line up two magnets so that the south pole of one faces the north pole of the other, the magnets will pull toward each other. This is called attraction.
