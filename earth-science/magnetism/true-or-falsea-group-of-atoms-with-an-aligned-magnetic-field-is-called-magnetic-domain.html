TRUE OR FALSE:A group of atoms with an aligned magnetic field is called magnetic domain

(A) ** TRUE

(B) FALSE

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Each atom has its own magnetic field, and if a group of atoms can align their magnetic fields, this group or cluster of atoms is called a magnetic domain.

Concept note-2: -A magnetic domain is region in which the magnetic fields of atoms are grouped together and aligned. In the experiment below, the magnetic domains are indicated by the arrows in the metal material. You can think of magnetic domains as miniature magnets within a material.

Concept note-3: -A magnetic domain is a region within a magnetic material in which the magnetization is in a uniform direction. This means that the individual magnetic moments of the atoms are aligned with one another and they point in the same direction.

Concept note-5: -A grouping of atoms that have their magnetic fields lined up in the same direction is called a magnetic domain.
