the ____ of an object is the force acting on the object due to gravity

(A) ** weight

(B) mass

(C) volume

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer is the force that it exerts on the ground. In science and engineering, the weight of an object is the force acting on the object due to gravity. Weight as a vector quantity, the gravitational force acting on the object.

Concept note-2: -Weight ( ) is a force, measured in newtons ( ). Weight is caused by the pull of gravity acting on a mass, usually near the surface of a planet. The weight of an object can change if it is on a different planet, with a different gravitational field strength, .

Concept note-3: -The force of gravity acting on an object is called its weight.

Concept note-4: -The weight of an object is the force of gravity on the object and may be defined as the mass times the acceleration of gravity, w = mg. Since the weight is a force, its SI unit is the newton.

Concept note-5: -On Earth, the weight of the body (like any other object) is given by Newtonâ€™s laws as mass times gravitational acceleration (w = m * g). That is, the weight of an object is determined by the pull of gravity on it.
