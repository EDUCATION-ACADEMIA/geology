(A) replacing the wire with an insulator inside the solenoid

(B) increasing the temperature surrounding the solenoid

(C) ** increasing the number of wire coils in the solenoid

(D) removing the iron core from the solenoid

Concept note-1: -The strength of the magnetic field produced by the solenoid can be increased: By placing soft iron inside the solenoid. By increasing the number of turns of the coil.

Concept note-2: -An electromagnet is a solenoid wrapped around a bar of iron or other ferromagnetic material. It is stronger if there are more turns in the coil of wire or there is more current flowing through it. Concept note-3: -The greater the current, the greater the strength. This because the current increases there is more flow of electrons which in turn increases the magnetic field around it. (b) The number of turns is increased. The greater the number of coils, the greater the strength.

Concept note-4: -Increasing the number of coils adds more field lines and makes for a stronger electromagnetic field. As the number of loops of wire increases, the strength of the electromagnetic field increases. So, the strength of the field is affected by the number of coils, wire used, and the thickness of the wire.
