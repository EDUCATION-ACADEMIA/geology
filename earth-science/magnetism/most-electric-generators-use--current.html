Most electric generators use ____ current.

(A) ** alternating

(B) direct

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The output is alternating electrical power in the form of alternating voltage and current. AC generators work on the principle of Faraday’s law of electromagnetic induction, which states that electromotive force – EMF or voltage – is generated in a current-carrying conductor that cuts a uniform magnetic field.

Concept note-2: -The rotor generates a moving magnetic field around the stator, which induces a voltage difference between the windings of the stator. This produces the alternating current (AC) output of the generator.

Concept note-3: -Generators are capable of both AC and DC power. Alternators usually are made for A/C (alternating current) applications, hence the name “alternator."

Concept note-4: -Alternating current (AC) Induction AC motors may be used as generators, turning mechanical energy into electric current.

Concept note-5: -AC and DC power generators both use electromagnetic induction to generate electricity. However, the process they use is different. An AC generator creates an alternating current that periodically reverses direction. But in a DC generator, a direct current flows in one direction.
