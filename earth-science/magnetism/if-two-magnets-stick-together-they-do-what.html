If two magnets stick together, they do what?

(A) repel

(B) ** attract

(C) change

(D) retract

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The rule to remember is that opposites attract. Every magnet has both a north and a south pole. When you place the north pole of one magnet near the south pole of another magnet, they are attracted to one another.

Concept note-2: -Magnets can also attract each other, but only if they face in opposite directions. A magnet has two ends called poles; one end is the north pole and the other is the south pole. A north pole will attract a south pole; the magnets pull on each other. But the two north poles will push each other away.

Concept note-3: -Magnets are objects that produce magnetic fields and attract metals like iron, nickel and cobalt. The magnetic fieldâ€™s lines of force exit the magnet from its north pole and enter its south pole.

Concept note-4: -This is called attraction. If you line the magnets up so that two of the same poles face each other, the magnets will push away. This is called repulsion.

Concept note-5: -Every magnet has a north and a south pole. Two like poles will repel each other but opposites attract.
