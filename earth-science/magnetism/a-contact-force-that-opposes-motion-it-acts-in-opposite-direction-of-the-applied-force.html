A contact force that opposes motion. It acts in opposite direction of the applied force.

(A) Gravity

(B) Force

(C) ** Friction

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The frictional force opposes the motion of an object relative to its surface in contact. However, the direction of frictional force may be the same as the direction of motion of the object. For example, during walking we apply a force in the backward direction with our foot.

Concept note-2: -Frictional force offers the resistance to the applied force opposing its motion. Thus, it always acts in the direction opposite to that of the object in motion. If force is applied to the left, then friction acts in the right.

Concept note-3: -Friction is a force that opposes motion. When two objects are in contact, friction is acts in a direction opposite to the motion of the object.

Concept note-4: -The force of friction is the resistance of motion that arises when two objects rub against each other. Hence, this force always acts in the opposite direction of motion of an object.

Concept note-5: -Friction always acts in the opposite direction to movement, i.e. it opposes the motion of an object. Air resistance is a frictional force which opposes motion through air.
