To increase the strength of an electromagnet you should

(A) ** increase the number of coils in the solenoid and / or increase the voltage (source)

(B) decrease the number of coils in the solenoid and / or increase the voltage (source)

(C) increase the number of coils in the solenoid and / or decrease the voltage (source)

(D) decrease the number of coils in the solenoid and / or decrease the voltage (source)

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The strength of an electromagnet can be increased by increasing the number of loops of wire around the iron core and by increasing the current or voltage.

Concept note-2: -We can say that, the strength of the electromagnet is directly proportional to the number of turns in the coil. If you double the number of turns, you double the strength of the electromagnet.

Concept note-3: -what happens to the strength of an electromagnet when the number of coils in the solenoid is decreased? is decreased, the strength of the electromagnet will decrease. This is a modal window.

Concept note-4: -Increasing the number of coils adds more field lines and makes for a stronger electromagnetic field. As the number of loops of wire increases, the strength of the electromagnetic field increases.

Concept note-5: -Increase the number of coil turns. Increase the current flowing through the coil. Wrap the coil tightly around the iron piece. Increase the current or voltage.
