[SOURCES]
Which of the following is not a DC source?

(A) batteries

(B) solar cells

(C) generators

(D) ** wall sockets

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -DC Circuit Types Every electrical circuit can be classified into three groups – series, series-parallel and parallel. Thus, DC circuits can be divided into three categories: series DC circuit, series and parallel DC circuit, and parallel DC circuit.

Concept note-2: -When you plug things into the outlet in your house, you don’t get DC. Household outlets are AC-Alternating Current.

Concept note-3: -Some sources of direct current are cells, DC generators, solar cells, etc. Dry cell, dry cell battery, car battery, DC generator, radio, and television work only with DC. They have a device in them that converts AC supplied to them into DC.

Concept note-4: -A capacitor does not allow d.c. to pass through it but allows an a.c. whereas resistor and inductor allow both a.c. as well as d.c. to pass through them.
