The Paris Agreement saw HICs agree to what for the first time?

(A) Reduce greenhouse gas emissions

(B) ** Fund climate change initiatives in LICs/NEEs

(C) Pass the agreement into law

(D) That climate change was irrefutable

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Paris Agreement presents an action plan to limit global warming. Its main elements are: a long-term goal – governments agreed to keep the increase in global average temperature to well below 2°C above pre-industrial levels and to pursue efforts to limit it to 1.5°C.

Concept note-2: -The Paris Agreement’s central aim is to strengthen the global response to the threat of climate change by keeping a global temperature rise this century well below 2 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels and to pursue efforts to limit the temperature increase even further to 1.5 degrees Celsius.

Concept note-3: -The Kyoto Protocol [PDF], adopted in 1997 and entered into force in 2005, was the first legally binding climate treaty. It required developed countries to reduce emissions by an average of 5 percent below 1990 levels, and established a system to monitor countries’ progress.

Concept note-4: -It entered into force on 4 November 2016. Its overarching goal is to hold “the increase in the global average temperature to well below 2°C above pre-industrial levels” and pursue efforts “to limit the temperature increase to 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels.”
