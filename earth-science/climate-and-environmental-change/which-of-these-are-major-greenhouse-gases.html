Which of these are major greenhouse gases

(A) ** Carbon dioxide

(B) Hydrogen

(C) ** Methane

(D) ** Water vapor

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Water vapor is Earth’s most abundant greenhouse gas. It’s responsible for about half of Earth’s greenhouse effect-the process that occurs when gases in Earth’s atmosphere trap the Sun’s heat.

Concept note-2: -Greenhouse gases that occur both naturally and from human activities include water vapor, carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), nitrous oxide (N2O) and ozone (O3).

Concept note-3: -Notes. CO2 accounts for about 76 percent of total greenhouse gas emissions. Methane, primarily from agriculture, contributes 16 percent of greenhouse gas emissions and nitrous oxide, mostly from industry and agriculture, contributes 6 percent to global emissions.

Concept note-4: -Carbon dioxide (CO2) Methane. Nitrous oxide. Water vapour.
