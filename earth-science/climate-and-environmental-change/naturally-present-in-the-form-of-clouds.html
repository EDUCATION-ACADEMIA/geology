Naturally present in the form of clouds.

(A) Carbon dioxide

(B) ** Water Vapor

(C) Nitrous Oxide

(D) Oxygen

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Clouds form when the invisible water vapor in the air condenses into visible water droplets or ice crystals. For this to happen, the parcel of air must be saturated, i.e. unable to hold all the water it contains in vapor form, so it starts to condense into a liquid or solid form.

Concept note-2: -The process of water changing from a gas to a liquid is called “condensation, ” and when gas changes directly into a solid, it is called “deposition.” These two processes are how clouds form.

Concept note-3: -Water is constantly cycling through the atmosphere. Water evaporates from the Earth’s surface and rises on warm updrafts into the atmosphere. It condenses into clouds, is blown by the wind, and then falls back to the Earth as rain or snow.

Concept note-4: -If a volume of air contains its maximum amount of water vapor and the temperature is decreased, some of the water vapor will condense to form liquid water. This is why clouds form as warm air containing water vapor rises and cools at higher altitudes where the water condenses to the tiny droplets that make up clouds.
