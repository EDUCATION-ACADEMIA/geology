What percentage of the Earth’s atmosphere is Carbon Dioxide?

(A) 25%

(B) ** 0.04%

(C) 3%

(D) 29%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -CO2 makes up only about 0.04% of the atmosphere, and water vapor can vary from 0 to 4%. But while water vapor is the dominant greenhouse gas in our atmosphere, it has “windows” that allow some of the infrared energy to escape without being absorbed.

Concept note-2: -What’s in the Air? By volume, the dry air in Earth’s atmosphere is about 78.08 percent nitrogen, 20.95 percent oxygen, and 0.93 percent argon. A brew of trace gases accounts for the other approximately 0.04 percent, including the greenhouse gases carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide and ozone.

Concept note-3: -Carbon dioxide (CO2 ) constitutes about 0.03% (300 parts per million) of the atmospheric gases. Whereas, nitrogen comprises 70% and oxygen 21% of the atmospheric gases.

Concept note-4: -Carbon occupies about 0.03% of the earth’s atmosphere.

Concept note-5: -The proportion of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere is about 0.03% Q. About a hundred years ago, the proportion of carbon dioxide in air was about 0.029%. Today it is 0.035%.
