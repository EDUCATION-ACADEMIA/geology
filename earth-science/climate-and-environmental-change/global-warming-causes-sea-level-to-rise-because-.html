Global warming causes sea level to rise because ____

(A) Hotter things expand, and the warmer the ocean, the larger it gets.

(B) It prevents ice and glaciers from forming, slowing down how much water is removed from the ocean each winter.

(C) It causes glaciers and ice caps to melt, putting more water into our oceans.

(D) ** All of these answers.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sea level rise is caused primarily by two factors related to global warming: the added water from melting ice sheets and glaciers, and the expansion of seawater as it warms.

Concept note-2: -First, glaciers and ice sheets worldwide are melting and adding water to the ocean. Second, the volume of the ocean is expanding as the water warms. A third, much smaller contributor to sea level rise is a decline in the amount of liquid water on land-aquifers, lakes and reservoirs, rivers, soil moisture.

Concept note-3: -About 2 feet (0.6 meters) of sea level rise along the U.S. coastline is increasingly likely between 2020 and 2100 because of emissions to date. Failing to curb future emissions could cause an additional 1.5-5 feet (0.5-1.5 meters) of rise for a total of 3.5-7 feet (1.1-2.1 meters) by the end of this century.

Concept note-4: -Due to melting of polar ice and glaciers, there will be rise in sea level on coastal wet lands. It would raise worldwise sea level, thereby, many big cities in the coastal areas will be covered by sea water.
