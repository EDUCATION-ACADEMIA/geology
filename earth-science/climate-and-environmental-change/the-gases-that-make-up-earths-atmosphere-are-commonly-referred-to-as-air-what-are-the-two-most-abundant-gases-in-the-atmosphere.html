The gases that make up Earth’s atmosphere are commonly referred to as air. What are the two most abundant gases in the atmosphere?

(A) nitrogen and carbon dioxide

(B) ** nitrogen and oxygen

(C) water vapor and argon

(D) oxygen and carbon dioxide

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It’s a mixture of different gases. The air in Earth’s atmosphere is made up of approximately 78 percent nitrogen and 21 percent oxygen.

Concept note-2: -Gases. The most abundant naturally occurring gas is nitrogen (N2), which makes up about 78% of air. Oxygen (O2) is the second most abundant gas at about 21%. The inert gas argon (Ar) is the third most abundant gas at 0.93%.

Concept note-3: -The abundance of gases in the atmosphere in the decreasing order is as follows: nitrogen, oxygen, argon, water vapour, carbon dioxide. Hence nitrogen constitutes 78% of the atmospheric air and is the most abundant gas in the atmosphere.

Concept note-4: -By volume, the dry air in Earth’s atmosphere is about 78.08 percent nitrogen, 20.95 percent oxygen, and 0.93 percent argon. A brew of trace gases accounts for the other approximately 0.04 percent, including the greenhouse gases carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide and ozone.

Concept note-5: -The second most abundant gas in the atmosphere is carbon dioxide.
