The increase in burning of fossils fuels has led to an ____ in carbon dioxide in the atmosphere.

(A) ** increase

(B) decrease

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When fossil fuels are burned, they release large amounts of carbon dioxide, a greenhouse gas, into the air. Greenhouse gases trap heat in our atmosphere, causing global warming.

Concept note-2: -Over the last century, burning of fossil fuels like coal and oil has increased the concentration of atmospheric carbon dioxide (CO2). This increase happens because the coal or oil burning process combines carbon with oxygen in the air to make CO2.

Concept note-3: -Carbon dioxide in the atmosphere warms the planet, causing climate change. Human activities have raised the atmosphereâ€™s carbon dioxide content by 50% in less than 200 years.

Concept note-5: -Human activity has rapidly increased the emission of greenhouse gases to the atmosphere. Since the start of the Industrial Revolution, in about 1750, human activities such as burning fossil fuels, including coal and oil, have increased greenhouse gas concentrations in our atmosphere.
