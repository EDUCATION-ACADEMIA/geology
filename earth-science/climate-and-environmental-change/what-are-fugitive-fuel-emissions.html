What are fugitive fuel emissions?

(A) emissions from illegal burning of fossil fuels

(B) Emissions from countries not participating in the Kyoto protocol

(C) ** Emissions produced as a by-product of fuel production or transport

(D) Emissions from heavy polluters

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Fugitive emission is defined as the unintentional and undesirable emission, leakage or discharge of gases or vapors from pressure-containing equipment or facilities and components inside plants such as valves, piping flanges, pumps, storage tanks, valves, compressors, etc.

Concept note-2: -Fugitive emissions are mostly methane, a GHG with a global warming potential 25 times higher than carbon dioxide over a 100-year period. Methane is found in coal and oil deposits and is released during the mining or extraction process.

Concept note-3: -Fugitive emissions come in many forms including dust, fine particles, and aerosols. Of these, the most environmentally impactful fugitive emissions are greenhouse gases, such as refrigerants and methane.

Concept note-4: -Fugitive gas emissions are emissions of gas (typically natural gas, which contains methane) to atmosphere or groundwater which result from oil and gas or coal mining activity.
