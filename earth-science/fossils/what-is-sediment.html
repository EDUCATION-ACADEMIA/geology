What is sediment?:)

(A) ** A solid material that is moved and deposited in a new location

(B) Liquid

(C) An extremely thin coating of carbon on rock

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sediment is solid material that is moved and deposited in a new location. Sediment can consist of rocks and minerals, as well as the remains of plants and animals. It can be as small as a grain of sand or as large as a boulder. Sediment moves from one place to another through the process of erosion.

Concept note-2: -8 Sediments can be carried from one place to another. The movement of sediments by wind, water, ice, or gravity is called erosion.

Concept note-3: -Deposition is the laying down of sediment carried by wind, flowing water, the sea or ice. Sediment can be transported as pebbles, sand and mud, or as salts dissolved in water. Salts may later be deposited by organic activity (e.g. as sea shells) or by evaporation.

Concept note-4: -noun. sed·i·ment. ˈsed-ə-mənt. : the material from a liquid that settles to the bottom. : material (as stones and sand) deposited by water, wind, or glaciers.
