A dinosaur track, a trail, and burrow are all examples of

(A) casts

(B) molds

(C) ** trace fossils

(D) preserved remains

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Trace fossils are indicators of the activities and behaviors of past life. Examples of trace fossils are tracks and trails, borings, burrows, gastroliths and coprolites.

Concept note-2: -What can dinosaur footprints tell us? Dinosaur tracks are a type of trace fossil. These are evidence of an animalâ€™s activity when it was alive, but are not part of the animal itself.

Concept note-3: -Examples of trace fossils are tracks, trails, burrows, borings, gnawings, eggs, nests, gizzard stones, and dung.

Concept note-4: -Ichnofossils, also known as trace fossils, are geological records of the activities and behaviors of past life. Some examples include rock evidence of nests, burrows, footprints, and scat.

Concept note-5: -Tetrapod footprints, worm trails and the burrows made by clams and arthropods are all trace fossils. Perhaps the most spectacular trace fossils are the huge, three-toed footprints produced by dinosaurs and related archosaurs. These imprints give scientists clues as to how these animals lived.
