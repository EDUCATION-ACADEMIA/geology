How are sedimentary rocks formed?

(A) cooling and hardening of lava

(B) applied heat and pressure

(C) ** compacting and cementing of sediments

(D) melting of rock

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -1) Clastic (detrital) sedimentary rocks are composed of the solid products of weathering (gravel, sand, silt, and clay) cemented together by the dissolved weathering products.

Concept note-2: -Pieces of rock are loosened by weathering, then transported to some basin or depression where sediment is trapped. If the sediment is buried deeply, it becomes compacted and cemented, forming sedimentary rock.

Concept note-3: -This process is called cementation. After compaction and cementation the sedimentary sequence has changed into a sedimentary rock.

Concept note-4: -Sedimentary rocks that form from sediments are called “clastic rocks. “ Clastic rocks are rock fragments that are compacted and cemented together (Figure below).
