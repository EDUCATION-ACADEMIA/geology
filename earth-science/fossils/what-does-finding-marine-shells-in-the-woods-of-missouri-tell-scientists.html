What does finding marine shells in the woods of Missouri tell scientists?

(A) Marine species once lived on land.

(B) ** The area of Missouri was once underwater.

(C) The fossils had most likely been transported from a marine environment to Pennsylvania.

(D) Marine organisms have adapted to survive on land.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Mississippian Period – 359 to 325 million years ago During Mississippian time, Missouri was covered in a shallow sea, much like the area near the Bahamas today.

Concept note-2: -During the early Paleozoic, Missouri was covered by a warm shallow sea that would come to be home to creatures like Archimedes, brachiopods, shelled cephalopods, conodonts, corals, crinoids, armored fishes, and trilobites.

Concept note-3: -(The twelfth bone has since been reunited with the others.) On Nov. 22, 2021, Museum Curator Guy Darrough formally announced the discovery of an adult Parrosaurus missouriensis fossil at the Chronister site. Darrough, director of the dig site, said the fossil and its surrounding material weighed 2, 500 pounds.
