During a Landslide, what should a person do?

(A) ** Move quickly away from its likely path

(B) ** Take shelter under a strong table

(C) Look for warping in your home

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Take cover under a sturdy table or other pieces of furniture, and hold on until the shaking stops. Stay away from glass, windows, outside doors and walls, and anything that could fall, such as lighting fixtures or furniture. Stay inside until the shaking stops, and it is safe to go outside.

Concept note-2: -Move quickly away from its likely path, keeping clear of embankments, trees, power lines and poles. Stay away from the landslide. The slope may experience additional failures for hours to days afterwards.

Concept note-3: -Evacuate. Getting out of the path of a landslide or debris flow is your best protection. Curl into a tight ball and protect your head if escape is not possible.

Concept note-4: -What should I do if a landslide or mudslide is occurring or likely to occur? If you suspect imminent danger, evacuate immediately. Inform affected neighbors if you can, and contact your public works, fire or police department.
