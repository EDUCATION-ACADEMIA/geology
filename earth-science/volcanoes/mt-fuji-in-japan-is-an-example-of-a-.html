Mt. Fuji in Japan is an example of a ____

(A) Shield volcano

(B) ** Composite volcano

(C) Cinder Cone volcano

(D) Mountain not a volcano

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The 3, 776-meter-high (12, 388 feet) Mount Fuji Volcano, located on the island of Honshu in Japan, is one of the worldâ€™s classic examples of a stratovolcano.

Concept note-2: -Mt. Fuji is one of the few large composite volcanoes made of basalt in the world. Its current beautiful cone shape was caused by eruptions during three periods: Komitake, Kofuji, and Shinfuji. An explosive Edo Period eruption 1707 created Hoei Crater and formed the huge volcanic ash field on the east side.

Concept note-3: -An example of a composite volcano is Mount Fuji. Shield volcanoes are broad dome shaped formations found along divergent boundaries. The viscosity is low which makes the basaltic lava runny. The silica content is low and the temperature is very high.

Concept note-4: -No, Mount Fuji is a composite volcano (or stratovolcano) due to its multiple layers of varied materials that have built up from previous eruptions.

Concept note-5: -Mt. Fujiyama in Japan is an example of volcanic mountains.
