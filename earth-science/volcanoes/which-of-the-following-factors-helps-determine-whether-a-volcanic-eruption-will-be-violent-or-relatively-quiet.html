Which of the following factors helps determine whether a volcanic eruption will be violent or relatively quiet?

(A) amount of dissolved gases

(B) temperature of the magma

(C) amount of silica in the magma

(D) ** all of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The amount of dissolved gas in the magma provides the driving force for explosive eruptions. The viscosity of the magma, however, is also an important factor in determining whether an eruption will be explosive or nonexplosive.

Concept note-2: -The two main factors that influence how a volcano will erupt are viscosity and gas content. Both are related to the composition of the magma. Hawaiian volcanoes tend to erupt basalt, which is low in viscosity and low in gas content (about 0.5 weight percent).

Concept note-3: -The style of eruption depends on a number of factors, including the magma chemistry and content, temperature, viscosity, volume and how much water and gas is in it, the presence of groundwater, and the plumbing of the volcano. gases and lava.

Concept note-4: -A more viscous (thick) magma will produce a more violent eruption This is controlled in part by the concentration of silica in the magma.
