What is it called when vibrations on Earth’s surface cause a break in Earth’s surface?

(A) ** Earthquakes

(B) Landslides

(C) Volcanic Eruptions

(D) Floods

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -As discussed in Lesson 5, earthquakes occur when elastic energy is accumulated slowly within the Earth’s crust as a result of plate motions and then released suddenly at fractures in the crust called faults. The released energy travels in the form of waves called seismic waves .

Concept note-2: -An earthquake is the vibration, sometimes violent, of the Earth’s surface that follows a release of energy in the Earth’s crust.

Concept note-3: -Earthquakes are usually caused when underground rock suddenly breaks and there is rapid motion along a fault. This sudden release of energy causes the seismic waves that make the ground shake.

Concept note-4: -Strong vibrations on the surface of the Earth is called an earthquake.

Concept note-5: -The primary effects of earthquakes are ground shaking, ground rupture, landslides, tsunamis, and liquefaction.
