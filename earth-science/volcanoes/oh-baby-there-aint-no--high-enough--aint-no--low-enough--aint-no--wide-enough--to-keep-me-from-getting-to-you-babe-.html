Oh baby, there ain’t no ____ high enough, / Ain’t no ____ low enough, / Ain’t no ____ wide enough / To keep me from getting to you babe ____

(A) tower, gutter, river

(B) volcano, valley, stream

(C) ** mountain, valley, river

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It’s a love song, but it’s actually Bible-inspired. It can be used to connect us to our true Lover, and not just any human lover. The idea behind is that whatever happens in life, in the high moments or the lows, God is always there for us. We have no reason to worry.

Concept note-2: -When “Ain’t No Mountain High Enough” was released by Tammi Terrell and Marvin Gaye in 1967, the single peaked at No. 19 on Billboard’s pop chart.

Concept note-3: -In 1966, Terrell then started a romance with The Temptations lead singer David Ruffin. That year, Terrell accepted Ruffin’s marriage proposal. After Terrell announced their engagement onstage, she discovered that he was already married. Ruffin had a wife, three children, and another girlfriend in Detroit.
