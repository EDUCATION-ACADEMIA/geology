What forms when hot water and steam are trapped underground in a narrow crack, get heated and erupt from the cracks?

(A) hot bath

(B) ** geyser

(C) lava plateau

(D) volcano

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A geyser forms when hot water and steam are trapped underground in a narrow crack. Pressure builds up until the hot water and steam erupt from the ground. This happens over and over again. Old Faithful is a geyser in Yellowstone National Park.

Concept note-2: -Subsurface magma heats groundwater, creating steam and hot water. The hot, less dense water rises through fissures and cracks in the ground. When it reaches the surface, features such as geysers, fumaroles, hot springs, and mud pits are created.

Concept note-3: -"Phreatic‚Äù (or steam-blast) eruptions are driven by explosive expanding steam resulting from cold ground or surface water coming into contact with hot rock or magma.

Concept note-4: -Fumaroles. Fumaroles are places where steam and volcanic gases are emitted. They are present on most active volcanoes. The occurrence of fumaroles and other geothermal features such as hot springs, geysers, and mud pots are important signs that a volcano is active.

Concept note-5: -What makes a geyser erupt? Water percolating down from above is warmed by geothermal heat from below, forming pressurized steam in an underground cavity. The high pressure causes the water to become superheated above its usual boiling point of 212 degrees F (100 degrees C).
