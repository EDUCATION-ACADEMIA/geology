Molten rocks on the surface of volcanoes are oozing out. Which volcanic material best describes this?

(A) Magma

(B) ** Lava

(C) Pyroclastic material

(D) Silica

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When magma is ejected by a volcano or other vent, the material is called lava. Magma that has cooled into a solid is called igneous rock. Magma is extremely hot-between 700° and 1, 300° Celsius (1, 292° and 2, 372° Fahrenheit).

Concept note-2: -Extrusive Igneous Rocks: These are the rocks that form at erupting volcanoes and oozing fissures. The magma, called lava when molten rock erupts on the surface, cools and solidifies almost instantly when it is exposed to the relatively cool temperature of the atmosphere.

Concept note-3: -Volcanic eruptions produce three types of materials: gas, lava, and fragmented debris called tephra.

Concept note-4: -Molten rock below the surface of the Earth that rises in volcanic vents is known as magma, but after it erupts from a volcano it is called lava.

Concept note-5: -When lava erupts it is made up of a slush of crystals, liquid, and bubbles. The liquid “freezes” to form volcanic glass. Chemically lava is made of the elements silicon, oxygen, aluminum, iron, magnesium, calcium, sodium, potassium, phosphorus, and titanium (plus other elements in very small concentrations.
