A shield volcano ____

(A) is built from layers of lava and volcanic ash.

(B) consists primarily of cinders and volcanic rock.

(C) ** composed almost entirely of thin lava.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Shield volcanoes are composed almost entirely of relatively thin lava and are built by many layers over time and the layers are usually of very similar composition. Hence, Option 1 is correct. Most shields were formed by low viscosity basaltic magma that flows easily downslope away from the summit vent.

Concept note-2: -Shield volcanoes are constructed primarily of thin lava flows that cover large areas. Basaltic shield volcanoes such as Mauna Loa and Kīlauea in Hawai’i Volcanoes National Park erupt primarily pāhoehoe and ‘a’ā lavas.

Concept note-3: -Shield volcanoes are built up of a great many flows of runny lava, each of which is relatively thin, only a few meters (yards) thick. Characteristic features of shield volcanoes are lava tubes, underground channels that fed the flows on the slopes of the volcano.

Concept note-4: -It is formed by the eruption of highly fluid (low viscosity) lava, which travels farther and forms thinner flows than the more viscous lava erupted from a stratovolcano. Repeated eruptions result in the steady accumulation of broad sheets of lava, building up the shield volcano’s distinctive form.

Concept note-5: -Shield volcanoes Where a volcano produces low viscosity, runny lava, it spreads far from the source and forms a volcano with gentle slopes: a shield volcano. Most shield volcanoes are formed from fluid, basaltic lava flows. Mauna Kea and Mauna Loa are shield volcanoes.
