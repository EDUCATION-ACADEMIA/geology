Which type of magma has the lowest gas content?

(A) ** Basaltic Magma

(B) Andesitic Magma

(C) Rhyolitic Magma

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Basaltic: Lastly, this type of magma has the lowest viscosity of all three magma types. It contains 45-55% silicon dioxide, so it is very fluid. The temperature of this magma is highest among the three different types, but its gas content is much less.

Concept note-2: -Answer and Explanation: Basaltic magma has the lowest viscosity because of its composition. Basaltic magma is mostly composed of iron, calcium, and magnesium, while half or less of it is composed of silica, potassium, and sodium.

Concept note-3: -Volumetrically, most lava is of basaltic composition. Basaltic melts have overall lower gas contents and are more fluid than their andesitic-to-rhyolitic counterparts.

Concept note-4: -Part A: Eruption of Less-Gassy Magma These rocks are called basalt (ba-SALT) and pumice (PUM-is).
