If the mineral’s chemical formula is PbS (Lead sulfide), what mineral group is it in?

(A) ** sulfides

(B) sulfates

(C) halides

(D) carbonates

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sulphides are minerals with the S–2 anion, and they include galena (PbS ), sphalerite (ZnS), chalcopyrite (CuFeS2), and molybdenite (MoS2), which are the most important ores of lead, zinc, copper, and molybdenum respectively.

Concept note-2: -sulfide mineral, sulfide also spelled sulphide, any member of a group of compounds of sulfur with one or more metals. Most of the sulfides are simple structurally, exhibit high symmetry in their crystal forms, and have many of the properties of metals, including metallic lustre and electrical conductivity.

Concept note-3: -galena, also called lead glance, a gray lead sulfide (PbS), the chief ore mineral of lead.

Concept note-4: -Galena is the natural mineral form of lead sulfide (PbS).
