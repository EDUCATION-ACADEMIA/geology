If the mineral’s chemical formula is BaSO 4 which mineral group is the mineral in?

(A) silicates

(B) carbonates

(C) halides

(D) ** sulfates

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The sulfate or sulphate ion is a polyatomic anion with the empirical formula SO2−4. Salts, acid derivatives, and peroxides of sulfate are widely used in industry.

Concept note-2: -Gypsum (CaSO4•2H2O) is the only common hydrous sulfate. Less common species include chalcanthite (CuSO4•5H2O), epsomite (MgSO4•7H2O), and antlerite Cu3SO4(OH)4.

Concept note-3: -Gypsum is a sulfate mineral composed of calcium sulfate dihydrate and has the chemical formula CaSO4 2H2O.

Concept note-4: -Barite or Barytes. Barite or Barytes; BaSO4. Celestite. Celestite; SrSO4. Anglesite. Anglesite; PbSO4. Gypsum. Gypsum; CaSO4·2H2O. Epsomite or Epsom Salt.
