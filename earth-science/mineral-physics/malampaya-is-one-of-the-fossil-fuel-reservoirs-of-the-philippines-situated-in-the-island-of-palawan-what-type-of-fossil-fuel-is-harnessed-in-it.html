Malampaya is one of the fossil fuel reservoirs of the Philippines situated in the island of Palawan. What type of fossil fuel is harnessed in it?

(A) Coal

(B) Coal and oil

(C) ** natural gas

(D) oil

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -MALAMPAYA | Power from the Deep » Technology. The Malampaya Deepwater Gas-to-Power project utilizes state-of-the-art technology to extract natural gas and condensate from the depths of the Palawan basin.

Concept note-2: -Malampaya derives natural gas from the Camago-Malampaya reservoir, a gigantic reserve of natural gas discovered in 1992 by Shell Philippines Exploration B.V. (SPEX).

Concept note-3: -The Malampaya gas field or the Malampaya-Camago field is a deepwater gas-condensate reservoir, located in the Service Contract 38 license area, located offshore, 65 km northwest of the island of Palawan, Philippines.

Concept note-4: -Since it began operations in 2001, the Malampaya project has produced cleaner-burning natural gas which supplies four power plants in Luzon, the country’s largest island, with a combined capacity of 3, 200 megawatts.

Concept note-5: -The country produces oil, natural gas, and coal. Geothermal, hydropower, and other renewable sources account for a significant share of electricity generation.
