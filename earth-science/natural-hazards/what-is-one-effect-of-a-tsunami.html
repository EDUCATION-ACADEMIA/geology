what is one effect of a tsunami?

(A) ** flooding

(B) weathering

(C) an earthquake

(D) a volcanic eruption

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There are three factors of destructions from tsunamis: inundation, wave impact on structures, and erosion. Strong, tsunami-induced currents lead to the erosion of foundations and the collapse of bridges and seawalls.

Concept note-2: -In addition to loss of life and mass injuries, other potential impacts include damage to and destruction of homes and businesses, ports and harbors, cultural resources, utilities, and critical infrastructure and facilities. There may be loss of access to basic services such as power, sewer, and water.

Concept note-3: -Tsunamis may create significant flooding in coastal locations. A large tsunami might inundate areas many kilometres inland. The waves that impact the shore first, followed by floods inland, can inflict significant infrastructure damage (including houses and roads), land, agriculture, and livestock.

Concept note-4: -People who live in floodplains or non-resistant buildings, or lack warning systems and awareness of flooding hazard, are most vulnerable to floods.

Concept note-5: -A tsunami is a series of enormous waves created by an underwater disturbance usually associated with earthquakes occurring below or near the ocean. Volcanic eruptions, submarine landslides, and coastal rock falls can also generate a tsunami, as can a large asteroid impacting the ocean.
