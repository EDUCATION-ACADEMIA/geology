Which type of map shows the elevation of an area?

(A) mountain maps

(B) political maps

(C) ** topographic maps

(D) climate maps

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Topographic maps show the elevation of different landforms above sea level.

Concept note-2: -The distinctive characteristic of a topographic map is the use of elevation contour lines to show the shape of the Earthâ€™s surface. Elevation contours are imaginary lines connecting points having the same elevation on the surface of the land above or below a reference surface, which is usually mean sea level.

Concept note-3: -Topographical maps, also known as general purpose maps, are drawn at relatively large scales. These maps show important natural and cultural features such as relief, vegetation, water bodies, cultivated land, settlements, and transportation networks, etc.

Concept note-4: -Topographic maps are also called toposheets.

Concept note-5: -Typical example of a land surface represented by a topographic map. The contour interval is 5 feet, and the highest point (elevation 760+ feet) is located near the center of the map.
