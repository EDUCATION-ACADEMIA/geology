Which statement is true of earthquakes?

(A) All of them can be prevented.

(B) Weak ones can be prevented.

(C) Some of them can be prevented.

(D) ** None of them can be prevented.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -We cannot prevent natural earthquakes from occurring but we can significantly mitigate their effects by identifying hazards, building safer structures, and providing education on earthquake safety.

Concept note-2: -Avoid windows and doors, glass panels, and outside entrances. Ans. Examining and interpreting seismic data to forecast alarms in advance. Enacting legislation requiring only earthquake-resistant structures.

Concept note-3: -One must stay away from bookcases, furniture, and heavy appliances that may fall. The shelter must be under a table or bed, and hold on to a post or any other fixture. If one is outdoors, the shelter must be taken in a clear stop away from buildings, trees, and power lines.

Concept note-4: -No. Neither the USGS nor any other scientists have ever predicted a major earthquake. We do not know how, and we do not expect to know how any time in the foreseeable future.
