The scale used to measure the magnitude of an earthquake is? (CHOOSE ALL THAT APPLY)

(A) ** Richter Scale

(B) ** Mercalli scale

(C) Fujita Scale

(D) ** Moment Magnitude Scale

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Richter scale measures magnitude of earthquake whereas Mercalli scale is used to measure the intensity of earthquake. 2.

Concept note-2: -The Richter Scale It used a formula based on the amplitude of the largest wave recorded on a specific type of seismometer and the distance between the earthquake and the seismometer.

Concept note-3: -Today, earthquake magnitude measurement is based on the Moment Magnitude Scale (MMS). MMS measures the movement of rock along the fault. It accurately measures larger earthquakes, which can last for minutes, affect a much larger area, and cause more damage.

Concept note-4: -The moment magnitude provides an estimate of earthquake size that is valid over the complete range of magnitudes, a characteristic that was lacking in other magnitude scales.
