NATURAL HAZARDS

[SOURCES]
Rock underground suddenly breaks along a fault causing tectonic movement, causing what natural hazard?

(A) Volcanic Eruption

(B) Cyclone

(C) ** Earthquake

(D) Hurricane

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sol. The correct answer is option ”c”. Earthquakes are usually caused when rock underground suddenly breaks along a fault. This sudden release of energy causes the seismic waves that make the ground shake.

Concept note-2: -There are two types of earthquakes: tectonic and volcanic earthquakes. Tectonic earthquakes are produced by sudden movement along faults and plate boundaries. Earthquakes induced by rising lava or magma beneath active volcanoes is called volcanic earthquakes.

Concept note-3: -As time goes by, the movement of the plates causes the rocks around the fault to bend and stretch. That, in turn, causes the rocks to store energy, like compressed springs. Eventually, friction across the fault isn’t able to hold the rocks back, and a crack is created across the fault as it begins to slip.

Concept note-4: -Faults are cracks in the earth’s crust along which there is movement. These can be massive (the boundaries between the tectonic plates themselves) or very small. If tension builds up along a fault and then is suddenly released, the result is an earthquake.

Concept note-5: -An earthquake is what happens when two blocks of the earth suddenly slip past one another. The surface where they slip is called the fault or fault plane.
