Sedimentary rock formed from remains of plants and animals is called ____ ? ____

(A) chemical sedimentary rock

(B) ** organic sedimentary rock

(C) clastic sedimentary rock

(D) erosion

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Organic sedimentary rocks are formed as a result of the decomposition of dead plants and animals. It contains fossils and some examples include chalk, limestone, coal, and dolomite.

Concept note-2: -Organic detrital rocks form when parts of plants and animals decay in the ground, leaving behind biological material that is compressed and becomes rock. Coal is a sedimentary rock formed over millions of years from compressed plants.

Concept note-3: -Organic-rich sedimentary rocks are a specific type of sedimentary rock that contains significant amounts (>3%) of organic carbon. The most common types include coal, lignite, oil shale, or black shale.

Concept note-4: -Organic sedimentary rocks form from the accumulation and lithification of organic debris, such as leaves, roots, and other plant or animal material. Rocks that were once swampy sediments or peat beds contain carbon and are black, soft, and fossiliferous.
