SEDIMENTS

[SOURCES]
Natural gas is a flammable material that (select all that apply):

(A) ** found near oil deposits

(B) made of ice

(C) melts

(D) ** is without definite form

(E) ** produced from dead marine (ocean) organisms buried under layers of sediment

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Fossil Fuels Vocabulary A flammable material, without a definite form, produced from organic material (remains of marine organisms) buried under layers of sediment found near oil deposits.

Concept note-2: -Methane is an odorless, colorless, flammable gas. It is used primarily as fuel to make heat and light. It is also used to manufacture organic chemicals. Methane can be formed by the decay of natural materials and is common in landfills, marshes, septic systems and sewers.

Concept note-3: -In the United States and in a few other countries, natural gas is produced from shale and other types of sedimentary rock formations by forcing water, chemicals, and sand down a well under high pressure.

Concept note-4: -Petroleum, also called crude oil, is a fossil fuel. Like coal and natural gas, petroleum was formed from the remains of ancient marine organisms, such as plants, algae, and bacteria.
