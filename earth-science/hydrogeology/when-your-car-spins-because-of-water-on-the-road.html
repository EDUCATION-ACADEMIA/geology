[SOURCES]
When your car spins because of water on the road

(A) flooding

(B) ** hydroplane

(C) hydrophobia

(D) hydroelectric

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When the amount of water is too much and cannot be displaced by the tyre, it leads to hydroplaning of the vehicle. While driving on waterlogged roads or on wet roads after a light rainfall, you might feel your car’s tyres drifting away all of a sudden.

Concept note-2: -Hydroplaning happens when the tires on your vehicle lose their grip on a road surface and instead travel on a film of water sitting on top of the road. Hydroplaning dramatically reduces a driver’s ability to steer and brake.

Concept note-3: -Hydroplaning happens when a sheet of water comes between your tires and the pavement, causing your vehicle to lose traction and sometimes even spin out of control. It’s most likely to happen in the first few minutes of a light rain, when the rain mixes with oil residue on the road, creating slippery conditions.

Concept note-4: -Take your foot off the accelerator immediately after hydroplaning. Gently turn the steering wheel of your car in the hydroplaning direction. Wait until you can feel the tyres reconnect with the surface of the road. More items •21-Jul-2016

Concept note-5: -Immediately take your foot off of the accelerator. Although it may seem contradictory, gently turn your steering wheel in the direction your car is hydroplaning. Wait to feel the tires reconnect with the surface of the road. More items
