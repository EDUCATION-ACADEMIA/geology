During a period of drought, members of a community may volunteer to water their lawns every other day, rather than daily. The most important benefit of this action is-

(A) It fertilizes the soil

(B) It reduces air pollution

(C) ** It conserves the groundwater supply

(D) It adds nitrogen to the soil

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The United States Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) defines point source pollution as any contaminant that enters the environment from an easily identified and confined place. Examples include smokestacks, discharge pipes, and drainage ditches.

Concept note-2: -Pollution originating from a single, identifiable source, such as a discharge pipe from a factory or sewage plant, is called point-source pollution.

Concept note-3: -Human activities that generate domestic sewage and toxic waste cause water pollution by contaminating water with disease-causing microorganisms and poisonous substances. Oil spills are another source of water pollution that have devastating impacts on surrounding ecosystems.

Concept note-4: -The main point source of pollution to water is from sewage and waste water treatment, while for diffuse pollution, main sources are from farming and fossil fuel power plants (via the air).
