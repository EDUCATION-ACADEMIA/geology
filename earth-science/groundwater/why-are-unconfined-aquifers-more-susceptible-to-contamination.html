Why are unconfined aquifers more susceptible to contamination?

(A) Because they are under a layer of clay

(B) ** Because they are closer to the surface and not protected by a layer of clay

(C) Both of the previous

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Groundwater in an unconfined aquifer (some-times called a “water table aquifer”) is more vulnerable to contamination from surface pollution than a confined aquifer because pollutants on the land surface can enter the unconfined aquifer as water infiltrates the soil.

Concept note-2: -Unconfined aquifers generally are much more vulnerable to contamination than confined aquifers. For a well in a confined aquifer, the farther the well is from the unconfined area, the less vulnerable it is to contamination. Generally, the deeper the well, the less vulnerable it is to contamination.

Concept note-3: -Unconfined sand and gravel aquifers are more vulnerable to contamination.

Concept note-4: -which is more likely to be contaminated? an unconfined aquifer recharges quicker because a confined one cannot be reached by falling water because it can’t penetrate the impermeable layer of rock surrounding it.

Concept note-5: -Unconfined aquifers form at a quicker rate compared to confined aquifers. This is because they are in closer proximity to water sources from rain, streams or rivers. By contrast, confined aquifers are fed by underground tributaries.
