The release of moisture by living plants.

(A) evaporation

(B) precipitation

(C) condensation

(D) ** transpiration

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -This process of elimination of excess water from the plant body is known as transpiration. It is generally the evaporation of water from the surface of the leaves. During the process of transpiration, water molecules in the plant tissues are removed from the aerial parts of the plants.

Concept note-2: -Transpiration occurs when plants take up liquid water from the soil and release water vapor into the air from their leaves.

Concept note-3: -Transpiration is the evaporation of water from plants occurring at the leaves while their stomata are open for the passage of CO2 and O2 during photosynthesis.

Concept note-4: -The water eventually is released to the atmosphere as vapor via the plantâ€™s stomata-tiny, closeable, pore-like structures on the surfaces of leaves. Overall, this uptake of water at the roots, transport of water through plant tissues, and release of vapor by leaves is known as transpiration.

Concept note-5: -Thus, during transpiration, plants release water vapour into the atmosphere.
