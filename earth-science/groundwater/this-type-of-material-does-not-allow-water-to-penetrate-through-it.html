GROUNDWATER

[SOURCES]
This type of material does not allow water to penetrate through it.

(A) permeable

(B) ** impermeable

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Clay, shale and slate are rocks that do not allow water to pass through and are therefore classified as impermeable.

Concept note-2: -Impermeable Material means material that is impenetrable by water and includes building coverage, asphalt, concrete, and brick, stone, and wood that do not have permeable spacing.

Concept note-3: -An impermeable surface is any solid surface that will not allow water to penetrate through such as asphalt, concrete, stone, brick, roofing, or extremely compacted ground like a soccer field.

Concept note-4: -Clay and granite are impermeable, meaning that water cannot pass through. When water reaches the impermeable materials it is trapped and forms the water table – where we get our water.

Concept note-5: -Common materials that are considered impermeable include glass, plastic, asphalt, concrete, and clay.
