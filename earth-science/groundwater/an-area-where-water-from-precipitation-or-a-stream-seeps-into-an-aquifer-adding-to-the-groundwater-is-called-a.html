An area where water (from precipitation or a stream) seeps into an aquifer, adding to the groundwater, is called a

(A) water table

(B) ** recharge area

(C) discharge area

(D) runoff

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An area where permeable soil or rock allows water to seep into the ground to replenish an aquifer.

Concept note-2: -Infiltration happens when water soaks into the soil from the ground level. It moves underground and moves between the soil and rocks.

Concept note-3: -A recharge area is the place where water is able to seep into the ground and refill an aquifer because no confining layer is present. Recharge areas are necessary for a healthy aquifer.

Concept note-4: -The area in which water enters an aquifer .

Concept note-5: -This rock wall represents an “outcrop” (or location where the rock intersects the land surface) of an aquifer. The point where the ground water emerges into view is called a “seep” or “spring".
