The top boundary of the zone of saturation in an aquifer which can fluctuate based on precipitation and the amount of draw from wells

(A) aquifer

(B) ** water table

(C) watershed

(D) drainage system

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The water table is the boundary between the unsaturated zone and the saturated zone underground.

Concept note-2: -The upper surface of this water-filled area, or “zone of saturation", is called the water table.

Concept note-3: -The upper surface of this zone of saturation is called the water table. The saturated zone beneath the water table is called an aquifer, and aquifers are huge storehouses of water. What you are looking at in this picture is a “well” that exposes the water table, with an aquifer beneath it.

Concept note-4: -UNCONFINED AQUIFER: An aquifer in which the upper boundary is the top of the water table. PERMEABLE LAYER: A portion of the aquifer that contains porous rock materials that allow water to penetrate freely.

Concept note-5: -zone of saturation, saturated zone) is the area in an aquifer, below the water table, in which all pores, fractures, and cavities (including karst caves) are filled with water.
