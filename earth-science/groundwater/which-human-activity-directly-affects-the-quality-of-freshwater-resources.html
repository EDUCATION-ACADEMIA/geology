Which human activity directly affects the quality of freshwater resources?

(A) depleting the ozone layer

(B) burning gasoline in car engines

(C) throwing trash out of car windows

(D) ** allowing runoff from agricultural fields

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Urban and industrial development, farming, mining, combustion of fossil fuels, stream-channel alteration, animal-feeding operations, and other human activities can change the quality of natural waters.

Concept note-2: -There is considerable evidence that humans are responsible for disruptions and changes to local and global water cycles. A number of human activities can impact on the water cycle: damming rivers for hydroelectricity, using water for farming, deforestation and the burning of fossil fuels.

Concept note-3: -Major agricultural contributors to water pollution (and the main targets for water-pollution control) are nutrients, pesticides, salts, sediments, organic carbon, pathogens, metals and drug residues.
