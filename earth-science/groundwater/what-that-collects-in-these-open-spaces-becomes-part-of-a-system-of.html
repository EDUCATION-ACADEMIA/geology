What that collects in these open spaces becomes part of a system of?

(A) permeable

(B) impermeable

(C) aquifer

(D) ** groundwater

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Surface water is any body of water found on the Earthâ€™s surface, including both the saltwater in the ocean and the freshwater in rivers, streams, and lakes.

Concept note-2: -Groundwater is found in two zones. The unsaturated zone, immediately below the land surface, contains water and air in the open spaces, or pores. The saturated zone, a zone in which all the pores and rock fractures are filled with water, underlies the unsaturated zone.

Concept note-3: -Water that has travelled down from the soil surface and collected in the spaces between sediments and the cracks within rock is called groundwater. Groundwater fills in all the empty spaces underground, in what is called the saturated zone, until it reaches an impenetrable layer of rock.

Concept note-4: -Ground water is the water located underground that saturates the spaces between alluvial material (sand, gravel, silt, clay) or the crevices or fractures in rocks.
