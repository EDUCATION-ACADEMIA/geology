Overflow during a heavy rainstorm from a wastewater treatment plant would result in-

(A) over-treated drinking water.

(B) flooding in the local aquifer.

(C) ** polluted runoff into nearby ponds.

(D) weakened housing foundations.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -However, during heavy, prolonged rains, the amount of stormwater entering a combined sewer system can be overwhelming for the pipes and the treatment plant. This can lead to combined sewer overflows, or CSOs, which pollute rivers and streams to which they flow.

Concept note-2: -Overflow during a heavy rainstorm from a wastewater treatment plant would result in-over-treated drinking water.

Concept note-3: -When fertilizers enter surface water, they cause problems in the watershed by-clogging narrow streams and preventing water from flowing properly.

Concept note-4: -Runoff from agricultural land carries chemicals from fertilizers that collect in a lake. The buildup of chemicals can eventually cause-rainfall levels to drop causing drought and a shorter growing season. river tributaries to reroute, changing the flow of natural waterways.
