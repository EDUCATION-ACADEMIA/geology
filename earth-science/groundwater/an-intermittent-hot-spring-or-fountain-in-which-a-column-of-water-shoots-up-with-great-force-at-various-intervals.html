an intermittent hot spring or fountain in which a column of water shoots up with great force at various intervals

(A) Base Level

(B) Alluvium

(C) Well

(D) ** Geyser

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Geysers are hot springs that intermittently spout a column of hot water and steam into the air. This action is caused by the water in deep conduits beneath a geyser approaching or reaching the boiling point.

Concept note-2: -A geyser is a rare kind of hot spring that is under pressure and erupts, sending jets of water and steam into the air.

Concept note-3: -A geyser is a hot spring characterized by intermittent, turbulent discharges of boiling water and steam.

Concept note-4: -Geysers are a type of hot spring that jet out a column of boiling water up to several hundred meters in height at frequent intervals.

Concept note-5: -Hot springs: Pools of hydrothermally heated water. Geysers: Hot springs with constrictions in their plumbing, which causes them to periodically erupt to release the pressure that builds up.
