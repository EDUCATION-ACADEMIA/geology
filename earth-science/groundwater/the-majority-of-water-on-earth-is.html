[SOURCES]
The majority of water on Earth is

(A) ** Salt Water

(B) Lake Water

(C) Fresh Water

(D) Muddy Water

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Of all the water found on Earth, 97.3% is saltwater contained in oceans which is not suitable for direct human consumption. Rest 2.7% is freshwater, out of which 68.7% are ice sheets and glaciers and 30.1% is groundwater.

Concept note-2: -The vast majority of water on the Earth’s surface, over 96 percent, is saline water in the oceans. The freshwater resources, such as water falling from the skies and moving into streams, rivers, lakes, and groundwater, provide people with the water they need every day to live.

Concept note-3: -The oceans cover about 70 percent of the Earth’s surface, and that about 97 percent of all water on and in the Earth is saline-there’s a lot of salty water on our planet.

Concept note-4: -Salt in the ocean comes from two sources: runoff from the land and openings in the seafloor. Rocks on land are the major source of salts dissolved in seawater. Rainwater that falls on land is slightly acidic, so it erodes rocks.

Concept note-5: -Over 97 percent of the earth’s water is found in the oceans as salt water.
