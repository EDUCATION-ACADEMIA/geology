What type of organism recycles energy back into an ecosystem?

(A) Herbivore

(B) Carnivore

(C) Autotroph

(D) ** Decomposer

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Decomposers play a critical role in the flow of energy through an ecosystem. They break apart dead organisms into simpler inorganic materials, making nutrients available to primary producers.

Concept note-2: -Dead producers and consumers and their waste products provide matter and energy to decomposers. Decomposers transform matter back into inorganic forms that can be recycled within the ecosystem. So, the energy that enters an ecosystem as sunlight eventually flows out of the ecosystem in the form of heat.

Concept note-3: -Decomposers (fungi, bacteria, invertebrates such as worms and insects) have the ability to break down dead organisms into smaller particles and create new compounds. We use decomposers to restore the natural nutrient cycle through controlled composting.

Concept note-4: -Decomposers or saprotrophs recycle dead plants and animals into chemical nutrients like carbon and nitrogen that are released back into the soil, air and water.

Concept note-5: -Decomposers depend on the dead and decaying matter as their source of nourishment or energy. They draw nutrition by decomposing the complex organic matter in the dead remains of plant and animals into simple compounds and inorganic molecules. Q.
