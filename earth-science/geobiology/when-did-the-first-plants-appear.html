When did the first plants appear?

(A) in the paleogene

(B) in the Ordovician

(C) devoniarrean

(D) ** in silurian

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The first fossil record of the Vascular plants appeared in the second half of the Silurian period.

Concept note-2: -The oldest fossil proven to be a vascular land plant is Cooksonia, from the Pridolian Stage of the Silurian Period in Wales.

Concept note-3: -Most Silurian plant fossils have been assigned to the genus Cooksonia, a collection of branching-stemmed plants which produced sporangia at their tips. None of these plants had leaves, and some appear to have lacked vascular tissue.

Concept note-4: -Possibly the most remarkable biological event during the Silurian was the evolution and diversification of fish. Not only does this time period mark the wide and rapid spread of jawless fish, but also the appearances of both the first known freshwater fish and the first fish with jaws.
