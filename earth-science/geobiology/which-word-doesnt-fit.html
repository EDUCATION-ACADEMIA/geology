(A) biohazard

(B) ** asterisk

(C) biodome

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Typically, an asterisk is placed after a word or sentence that has a footnote attached.

Concept note-2: -Conventionally, an asterisk is six-pointed, although modern typefaces often have five-pointed or even four-pointed asterisks. In texts, an asterisk is used to mark footnotes and editor’s notes, to replace a title withheld, or to censor offensive language and omit unknown words.

Concept note-3: -The asterisk is a “wildcard” character that means “any text". The Word Document (*. docx) in the Save As Type box is just showing you that the document will be saved with the .

Concept note-4: -Asterisks are a way to: emphasize a word or a part of a sentence. make a word stand out of the context: I am not that kind of person *sighs* bullet-like list. call-out symbol* to footnotes.
