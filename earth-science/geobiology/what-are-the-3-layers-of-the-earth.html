What are the 3 layers of the earth

(A) crust, core mantle

(B) puclli mantle bark

(C) ** crust mantle core

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The interior of the earth can be divided into 3 different layers – crust, mantle, and core. The crust is the outermost layer of the earth, and the core is the innermost layer of the earth, located at a depth of 2900 Km.

Concept note-2: -The earth is made up of three different layers: the crust, the mantle and the core. This is the outside layer of the earth and is made of solid rock, mostly basalt and granite.

Concept note-3: -The mantle is divided into several layers: the upper mantle, the transition zone, the lower mantle, and D” (D double-prime), the strange region where the mantle meets the outer core. The upper mantle extends from the crust to a depth of about 410 kilometers (255 miles).

Concept note-4: -Answer: The Earth is made up of several layers. While the crust is its outermost layer, the interior of the Earth consists of the mantle, which is a 2, 900-km-thick layer lying beneath the Earth’s surface. The core is the innermost layer of the Earth, which lies below the mantle.

Concept note-5: -The structure of the earth is divided into four major components: the crust, the mantle, the outer core, and the inner core. Each layer has a unique chemical composition, physical state, and can impact life on Earth’s surface.
