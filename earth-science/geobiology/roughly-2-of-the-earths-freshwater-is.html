Roughly 2% of the earth’s freshwater is?

(A) ** frozen

(B) water vapor

(C) groundwater

(D) polluted water

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -About 2.1% of all of Earth’s water is frozen in glaciers. less than 1% is in all living plants and animals.

Concept note-2: -Over 68 percent of the fresh water on Earth is found in icecaps and glaciers, and just over 30 percent is found in ground water. Only about 0.3 percent of our fresh water is found in the surface water of lakes, rivers, and swamps.

Concept note-3: -About 10% of the land area on Earth is covered with glacial ice, in Greenland, Antarctica and high in mountain ranges; glaciers store much of the world’s freshwater.

Concept note-4: -Ice caps and global water distribution As these charts and the data table show, the amount of water locked up in ice and snow is only about 1.7 percent of all water on Earth, but the majority of total freshwater on Earth, about 68.7 percent, is held in ice caps and glaciers.

Concept note-5: -Antarctica is a continent capped by an inland ice sheet up to 4.8km thick, containing approximately 90% of the world’s total surface fresh water (and 60% of the world’s total fresh water).
