EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A geomorphic landscape is an area defined by a distinctive set of landforms produced by a distinctive set of geomorphic processes, for example, a riverine, arid or coastal landscape.

Concept note-2: -Geomorphology is the study of landforms and landform evolution. The topic traditionally has been studied both qualitatively, which is the description of landforms, and quantitatively, which is process-based and describes forces acting on Earthâ€™s surface to produce landforms and landform change.

Concept note-3: -Weathering, mass wasting, erosion and deposition are exogenic geomorphic processes. These exogenic processes are dealt with in detail in this chapter.

Concept note-4: -There are two main geomorphic processes; those are exogenic and endogenic processes.
