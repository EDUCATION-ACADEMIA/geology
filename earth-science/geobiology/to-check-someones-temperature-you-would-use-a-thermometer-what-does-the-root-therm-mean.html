To check someone’s temperature, you would use a thermometer. What does the root “therm” mean?

(A) ice

(B) sky

(C) ** heat

(D) see

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Thermometers are used to see if you have a fever or tell you how cold it is outside. Made up of thermo (heat) and meter (measuring device), the meaning of the word thermometer is pretty straightforward. Thermometers measure temperatures in degrees, according to either the Celsius or Fahrenheit system.

Concept note-2: –therm-, root.-therm-comes from Greek, where it has the meaning “heat. ‘’ This meaning is found in such words as: hypothermia, thermal, thermodynamics, thermometer, thermostat.

Concept note-3: -THERM means HEAT. Therm is the second root featured on the Resource Room found often in scientific contexts.

Concept note-4: –therm-comes from Greek, where it has the meaning “heat. ‘’ This meaning is found in such words as: hypothermia, thermal, thermodynamics, thermometer, thermostat.

Concept note-5: -How is temperature related to heat? Temperature is a measure of the heat of an object. Heat causes a change in the temperature of an object. Raising the temperature causes the heat of an object to increase.
