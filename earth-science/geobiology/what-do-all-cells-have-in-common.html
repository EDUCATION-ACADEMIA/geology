What do all cells have in common?

(A) ** plasma membrane

(B) ** cell membrane

(C) ** Cytoplasm

(D) Cellular wall

(E) vacuoles

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -All cells have a plasma membrane, cytoplasm, and ribosomes.

Concept note-2: -Parts common to all cells are the plasma membrane, the cytoplasm, ribosomes, and genetic material.

Concept note-3: -It is mainly composed of water, salts, and proteins. In eukaryotic cells, the cytoplasm includes all of the material inside the cell and outside of the nucleus. All of the organelles in eukaryotic cells, such as the nucleus, endoplasmic reticulum, and mitochondria, are located in the cytoplasm.

Concept note-4: -Cytoplasmic organelles are “little organs” that are suspended in the cytoplasm of the cell. Each type of organelle has a definite structure and a specific role in the function of the cell. Examples of cytoplasmic organelles are mitochondrion, ribosomes, endoplasmic reticulum, golgi apparatus, and lysosomes.
