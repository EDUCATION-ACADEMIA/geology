As the temperature of the ocean increases, which most likely occurs?

(A) ** the level of dissolved oxygen decreases

(B) the rate of photosynthesis increases

(C) the amount of minerals decreases

(D) the level of dissolved nitrogen increases

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -First, the solubility of oxygen decreases as temperature increases ยน. This means that warmer surface water requires less dissolved oxygen to reach 100% air saturation than does deeper, cooler water.

Concept note-2: -Dissolved oxygen, temperature, and aquatic life Cold water can hold more dissolved oxygen than warm water. In winter and early spring, when the water temperature is low, the dissolved oxygen concentration is high. In summer and fall, when the water temperature is high, the dissolved-oxygen concentration is often lower.

Concept note-3: -When water temperatures increase, it causes the gas and water molecules to gain more energy which in turn breaks the weak molecular interactions between water and oxygen molecules, causing the oxygen to escape.

Concept note-4: -Causes of Low Dissolved Oxygen Low dissolved oxygen (DO) primarily results from excessive algae growth caused by phosphorus. Nitrogen is another nutrient that can contribute to algae growth. As the algae die and decompose, the process consumes dissolved oxygen.

Concept note-5: -Ocean warming-driven deoxygenation: Warmer ocean water holds less oxygen and is more buoyant than cooler water. This leads to reduced mixing of oxygenated water near the surface with deeper waters, which naturally contain less oxygen. Warmer water also raises oxygen demand from living organisms.
