Which of these organisms is a producer in a marine ecosystem?

(A) fish

(B) gull

(C) ** algae

(D) worm

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Phytoplankton are the tiny, plant-like producers of the plankton community. They include bacteria and algae that form the base of aquatic food webs. Common phytoplankton include diatoms, dinoflagellates, cyanobacteria (blue-green algae), and green algae.

Concept note-2: -Producers. Primary producers-including bacteria, phytoplankton, and algae-form the lowest trophic level, the base of the aquatic food web. Primary producers synthesize their own energy without needing to eat.

Concept note-3: -Diatoms, the major phytoplanktons of the sea, are the chief producers of the ocean. They belong to the kingdom Protista.

Concept note-4: -Five producers in the ocean include an assortment of photosynthetic and chemosynthetic organisms. Seaweed, kelp, seagrass and phytoplankton are photosynthetic organisms that can create glucose as their own food source by using sunlight and water to convert carbon dioxide into glucose (a sugar molecule).

Concept note-5: -Most algae possess chlorophyll a. As primary producers, they use the sunlight energy to convert inorganic substances into simple organic compounds, and, provide the principal basis of food webs on the Earth.
