Which agent of erosion most likely formed the drumlins and finger lakes in New York State?

(A) running water

(B) ** moving ice

(C) wave action

(D) mass movement

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Abrasion by wind-carried sand is the leading cause of erosion in New York.

Concept note-2: -As the advancing ice deeply scoured former north-south river valleys it carved out the Finger Lakes in western New York State. In addition to producing features through erosion, ice sheets also produced features by deposition of unsorted glacial sediment (till).

Concept note-3: -Liquid water is the major agent of erosion on Earth. Rain, rivers, floods, lakes, and the ocean carry away bits of soil and sand and slowly wash away the sediment.

Concept note-4: -SEDIMENTARY PROCESSES | Glaciers Glacial erosion involves the removal and transport of bedrock or sediment by three main processes: quarrying (also known as plucking), abrasion, and melt water erosion.
