The continent of Antarctica is nearly covered with glaciers. Which statement is true about glaciers?

(A) Glaciers are frozen salt water.

(B) ** Glaciers are frozen fresh water.

(C) Glaciers contain a majority of the water on Earth.

(D) Glaciers contain the smallest fraction of water on Earth

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Glacier ice Worldwide, glaciers cover about 10% of the Earth’s land area, and Antarctica accounts for about 85% of this total cover. By volume, Antarctica contains 90% of the world’s glacier ice – enough ice to raise world sea level by almost 60 metres if it were all to melt.

Concept note-2: -About three-quarters of Earth’s freshwater is stored in glaciers. Therefore, glacier ice is the second largest reservoir of water on Earth and the largest reservoir of freshwater on Earth!

Concept note-3: -The amount of world’s freshwater in polar ice caps and glaciers is 70%.

Concept note-4: -Ice caps and global water distribution As these charts and the data table show, the amount of water locked up in ice and snow is only about 1.7 percent of all water on Earth, but the majority of total freshwater on Earth, about 68.7 percent, is held in ice caps and glaciers.
