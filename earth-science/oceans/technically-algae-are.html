Technically, algae are

(A) zooplankton

(B) bioplankton

(C) ** phytoplankton

(D) nektoplankton

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Phytoplankton, also known as microalgae, are similar to terrestrial plants in that they contain chlorophyll and require sunlight in order to live and grow. Most phytoplankton are buoyant and float in the upper part of the ocean, where sunlight penetrates the water.

Concept note-2: -Phytoplankton are the tiny, plant-like producers of the plankton community. They include bacteria and algae that form the base of aquatic food webs. Common phytoplankton include diatoms, dinoflagellates, cyanobacteria (blue-green algae), and green algae.

Concept note-3: -Algae and phytoplankton are not the same thing. There is, however, a degree of overlap between the two. Many algae, such as the diatoms, do indeed form part of phytoplankton. Other algae, such as red and brown algae, do not, as they are large multicellular organisms and are unable to float.

Concept note-4: -Scientists classify plankton in several ways, including by size, type, and how long they spend drifting. But the most basic categories divide plankton into two groups: phytoplankton (plants) and zooplankton (animals).
