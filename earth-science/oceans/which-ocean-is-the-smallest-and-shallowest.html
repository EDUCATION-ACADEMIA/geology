Which ocean is the smallest and shallowest?

(A) Pacific Ocean

(B) Atlantic Ocean

(C) Indian Ocean

(D) ** Arctic Ocean

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Arctic Ocean is the smallest of the world’s five ocean basins. A polar bear walks on the frozen surface of the Arctic Ocean.

Concept note-2: -The Arctic Ocean is the smallest and shallowest of the world’s five major oceans.

Concept note-3: -The Arctic Ocean is the shallowest (mean depth 1361 m) and has significantly larger continental shelves than other oceans. The deep central Arctic Ocean Basin consists of four abyssal plains separated by submarine ridges. The abyssal plains make 12 %, while ridges make 16 % of the total area of the Arctic Ocean.

Concept note-4: -The Continental Shelf is the shallowest part of the ocean. It is an extension of land that is submerged under the ocean.

Concept note-5: -The five oceans from smallest to largest are: the Arctic, Southern, Indian, Atlantic and Pacific.
