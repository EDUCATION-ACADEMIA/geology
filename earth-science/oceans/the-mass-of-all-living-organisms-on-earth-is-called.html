The mass of all living organisms on Earth is called

(A) nekton

(B) plankton

(C) benthos organisms

(D) ** biomass

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The biomass is the mass of living biological organisms in a given area or ecosystem at a given time. Biomass can refer to species biomass, which is the mass of one or more species, or to community biomass, which is the mass of all species in the community. It can include microorganisms, plants or animals.

Concept note-2: -Biomass is the mass of all living organisms in an ecosystem, or the mass of one or more species in an ecosystem, at a particular time.

Concept note-3: -Biomass energy, or “bioenergy, ” is the energy from plants and plant-derived materials.

Concept note-4: -Definition. Biomass refers to the mass of living organisms, including plants, animals, and microorganisms, or, from a biochemical perspective, cellulose, lignin, sugars, fats, and proteins.

Concept note-5: -Biomass, the contraction for biological IIIXS, is the amount of living material provided by a given area or volume of the earth’s surface, whether terrestrial or aquatic.
